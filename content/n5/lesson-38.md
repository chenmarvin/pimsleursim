### Word-by-word details

**1. 手続きする（てつづきする）— 辦手續**
- 例文: ビザの手続きをしました。
- 翻譯: 我辦了簽證的手續。
- Common mistake: Treating 手続き as a verb by itself (❌手続きます) — it's a noun that needs する, or the pattern 手続きをする.
- Memory tip: 手 (hand) + 続き (continuation) = "the steps you carry out by hand, one after another" — procedures.

**2. 申し込む（もうしこむ）— 申請**
- 例文: 大学に申し込みました。
- 翻譯: 我申請了大學。
- Common mistake: Confusing with 申す (humble "to say," not core vocab) — 申し込む specifically means "to submit an application/entry," not just "to state humbly."
- Memory tip: 申す (to state, humbly) + 込む (to put in/enter) = "to formally put in your request."

**3. 書類（しょるい）— 文件**
- 例文: この書類にサインしてください。
- 翻譯: 請在這份文件上簽名。
- Common mistake: Confusing 書類 (formal paperwork/documents) with 手紙 (Lesson 19, a personal letter) — 書類 is official paperwork, not correspondence.
- Memory tip: 書く (to write, Lesson 6) + 類 (kind/category) = "written materials of a kind" — documents.

**4. サイン — 簽名**
- 例文: ここにサインをお願いします。
- 翻譯: 請在這裡簽名。
- Common mistake: Assuming サイン means "sign" as in a road sign (that's 標識, Lesson 31) — as a loanword, サイン in Japanese narrows to mean only "signature/autograph."
- Memory tip: Loanword from English "sign," but narrowed to just "signature."

**5. 印鑑（いんかん）— 印章**
- 例文: 印鑑を持ってきてください。
- 翻譯: 請帶印章來。
- Common mistake: Assuming a handwritten signature always suffices as in Taiwan/the West — Japanese official documents often require 印鑑 (a carved personal seal) instead of or alongside a signature, which surprises many foreign residents at government offices.
- Memory tip: 印 (stamp/mark) + 鑑 (seal/mirror) = "the stamp used to mark [documents] as yours."

**6. 役所（やくしょ）— 公家機關**
- 例文: 役所で書類をもらいました。
- 翻譯: 我在公家機關拿到了文件。
- Common mistake: Confusing 役所 (a public/municipal office) with 会社 (Lesson 1, a private company) — 役所 always refers to government institutions.
- Memory tip: 役 (duty/service) + 所 (place) = "a place of public duty" — government office.

**7. 市役所（しやくしょ）— 市公所**
- 例文: 市役所で住民票をもらいます。
- 翻譯: 我要去市公所拿居住證明。
- Common mistake: Treating 市役所 and 役所 as the same thing — 役所 is the general category, while 市役所 specifically means the city-level office ("city hall"); other levels also exist (区役所, 町役場).
- Memory tip: 市 (city, Lesson 7) + 役所 (government office) = "the city's government office."

**8. 窓口（まどぐち）— 櫃檯**
- 例文: 2番の窓口へ行ってください。
- 翻譯: 請到2號櫃檯。
- Common mistake: Confusing 窓口 (a service counter where you interact with staff) with plain 窓 (window, Lesson 18, a literal window in a room) — 窓口 is a fixed compound meaning "point of contact/service counter."
- Memory tip: 窓 (window, Lesson 18) + 口 (mouth/opening, Lesson 12) = "the window-opening where you talk to staff."

**9. 記入する（きにゅうする）— 填寫**
- 例文: 名前をここに記入してください。
- 翻譯: 請把名字填在這裡。
- Common mistake: Confusing 記入する (to fill in a designated blank on a form) with general 書く (Lesson 6, "to write") — 記入する is specifically about filling in a form's blanks.
- Memory tip: 記 (record, from 記憶, Lesson 36) + 入 (enter, Lesson 16) = "entering a record [into a blank]."

**10. コピーする — 影印**
- 例文: この書類をコピーしてください。
- 翻譯: 請影印這份文件。
- Common mistake: Using コピーする to mean "imitating someone" (that's 真似する, not core vocab) — here it strictly means machine photocopying.
- Memory tip: A straightforward loanword from English "copy."

**11. ファックス — 傳真**
- 例文: ファックスで送ってください。
- 翻譯: 請用傳真寄送。
- Common mistake: Assuming fax machines are obsolete and skipping this word — in Japan, ファックス is still widely used in offices and government procedures, unlike in many other countries, so it's genuinely useful.
- Memory tip: Loanword from English "fax," pronounced ふぁっくす.

**12. 切手（きって）— 郵票**
- 例文: 切手を3枚買いました。
- 翻譯: 我買了三張郵票。
- Common mistake: Confusing 切手 (postage stamp) with 印鑑 (personal seal) or 標識 (road sign, Lesson 31) — three different "stamp/mark" concepts using unrelated words.
- Memory tip: 切る (to cut) + 手 (hand) = originally "a cut piece" — stamps were historically cut from sheets by hand.

**13. 封筒（ふうとう）— 信封**
- 例文: 手紙を封筒に入れました。
- 翻譯: 我把信放進信封裡了。
- Common mistake: Confusing 封筒 (envelope) with 袋 (ふくろ, Lesson 11, a generic bag) — 封筒 specifically refers to letter/document envelopes.
- Memory tip: 封 (seal shut) + 筒 (tube/cylinder) = "a sealed tube-like container" — envelope.

**14. 小包（こづつみ）— 包裹**
- 例文: 小包を出しに郵便局へ行きます。
- 翻譯: 我要去郵局寄包裹。
- Common mistake: Confusing 小包 (a mailed package/parcel) with 荷物 (Lesson 26, general luggage/baggage) — 小包 specifically refers to something sent by mail, not luggage you carry while traveling.
- Memory tip: 小 (small, Lesson 8) + 包む (to wrap, Lesson 11) = "a small wrapped thing" — parcel.

**15. 送料（そうりょう）— 運費**
- 例文: 送料はいくらですか。
- 翻譯: 運費多少錢？
- Common mistake: Confusing 送料 (the fee for sending/shipping something) with 手数料 (this lesson, a service/handling commission) — 送料 specifically covers shipping costs.
- Memory tip: 送る (to send, Lesson 19) + 料 (fee, from 料理, Lesson 29) = "the fee for sending."

**16. 振込（ふりこみ）— 匯款**
- 例文: 振込でお金を払いました。
- 翻譯: 我用匯款的方式付了錢。
- Common mistake: Confusing 振込 (a bank transfer, the noun form of 振り込む) with paying in 現金 (Lesson 11, cash) — these are different payment methods and are not interchangeable in a sentence.
- Memory tip: 振る (swing/shake) + 込む (put into) = an easy-to-picture image of "shaking money into" an account — transferring in.

**17. 口座（こうざ）— 帳戶**
- 例文: 銀行で口座を作りました。
- 翻譯: 我在銀行開了帳戶。
- Common mistake: Confusing 口座 (an abstract bank account) with 財布 (Lesson 2, wallet, a physical object you carry) — 口座 isn't something you can hold in your hand.
- Memory tip: 口 (mouth/opening, Lesson 12) + 座 (seat/place) = "a place [money] sits" — an account.

**18. 暗証番号（あんしょうばんごう）— 密碼**
- 例文: 暗証番号を忘れました。
- 翻譯: 我忘記密碼了。
- Common mistake: Confusing 暗証番号 (a secret banking/ATM PIN) with 電話番号 (Lesson 2, phone number) — both end in 番号, but you should never share your 暗証番号 the way you'd share a phone number.
- Memory tip: 暗 (dark/secret) + 証 (proof, this lesson's kanji) + 番号 (number, Lesson 2) = "the secret proof-number."

**19. 引き出す（ひきだす）— 提款**
- 例文: ATMでお金を引き出しました。
- 翻譯: 我在ATM提了錢。
- Common mistake: Confusing 引き出す (to withdraw money from an account) with a plain 出す (general "to take out/submit") — 引き出す is the specific banking term for withdrawal.
- Memory tip: 引く (pull) + 出す (take out) = "pulling money out" — withdraw.

**20. 預ける（あずける）— 存（款）、寄放**
- 例文: 銀行にお金を預けます。
- 翻譯: 我要把錢存進銀行。
- Common mistake: Confusing 預ける (to deposit/entrust something — money, luggage — to an institution for safekeeping) with 貸す (Lesson 22, "to lend" to a person) — 預ける doesn't imply lending, just leaving something safely somewhere.
- Memory tip: A natural opposite pair with 引き出す — 預ける (put in) vs. 引き出す (take out).

**21. 手数料（てすうりょう）— 手續費**
- 例文: 振込には手数料がかかります。
- 翻譯: 匯款需要手續費。
- Common mistake: Confusing 手数料 (a broad service/handling fee) with 送料 (this lesson, shipping fee specifically) — 手数料 covers any administrative/handling charge, not just shipping.
- Memory tip: 手数 (trouble/effort, literally "hand-count") + 料 (fee) = "a fee for the trouble [of handling something]."

**22. 保険証（ほけんしょう）— 健保卡**
- 例文: 病院で保険証を見せました。
- 翻譯: 我在醫院出示了健保卡。
- Common mistake: Confusing 保険証 (health insurance card) with 身分証明書 (this lesson, a general ID card) — 保険証 is specifically for health insurance, though it can double as informal ID in some contexts.
- Memory tip: 保険 (insurance) + 証 (proof, this lesson's kanji) = "proof of insurance."

**23. 身分証明書（みぶんしょうめいしょ）— 身分證**
- 例文: 身分証明書を見せてください。
- 翻譯: 請出示身分證。
- Common mistake: Confusing this general category term with パスポート (Lesson 26, passport, just *one* type of ID) — 身分証明書 covers passports, driver's licenses, and other ID documents alike.
- Memory tip: 身分 (identity/status) + 証明書 (certificate) = "a certificate proving your identity."

**24. 期限（きげん）— 期限**
- 例文: 申し込みの期限は今週の金曜日です。
- 翻譯: 申請的期限是這星期五。
- Common mistake: Confusing 期限 (a deadline, the final allowed date) with 予定 (Lesson 4, a schedule/plan) — 期限 marks a strict cutoff, not a general plan.
- Memory tip: 期 (period, from 期待する, Lesson 37) + 限 (limit) = "the limit of a period" — deadline.

**25. 遅れる（おくれる）— 遲、延誤**
- 例文: バスが遅れています。
- 翻譯: 巴士誤點了。
- Common mistake: Confusing 遅れる (broader "to be late/delayed" — applies to people, trains, deliveries, processes) with 遅刻する (Lesson 21, specifically "a person arriving late" to an appointment) — 遅れる is the wider word, not limited to people.
- Memory tip: Related to 遅い (slow, Lesson 17) — 遅れる = "to become late/behind."

## ② Kanji (2): 証, 名

**証 — Meaning: 證 (proof, certificate)**
- On-reading: ショウ
- Kun-reading: —
- Words: 保険証, 身分証明書, 暗証番号 — all from this lesson
- Example sentence: 保険証を見せてください。
- Stroke order: 12 strokes — write the 言 (speech) radical on the left first (point, horizontal strokes, then 口 at the bottom), then the 正 component on the right, top to bottom.
- Chinese difference: 証 is the shinjitai (simplified) form of Traditional Chinese 證, which Taiwanese learners write by hand — visually it's closer to Simplified Chinese 证. The meaning ("proof, certificate, witness") carries over directly.

**名 — Meaning: 名 (name)**
- On-reading: メイ、ミョウ
- Kun-reading: な
- Words: 名前 (なまえ, Lesson 1), 名刺 (めいし, Lesson 28)
- Example sentence: 名前をここに書いてください。
- Stroke order: 6 strokes — write 夕 (evening) on top first, then 口 (mouth) underneath; a helpful story: "at dusk (夕) it's too dark to see faces, so you call out (口) a person's name."
- Chinese difference: Identical character and meaning to Chinese 名 — fully transparent. The one thing to note is Japanese keeps two on-readings, メイ (common, as in 名前) and ミョウ (used in some compounds like 名字, みょうじ, "surname"), where Mandarin has just the single reading "míng."

## ③ Grammar (3 points)

### Grammar 1 — 〜なら、〜 ("if it's the case that...")
**Meaning:** A conditional built on a topic or premise already mentioned or assumed — often used to give advice tied to that specific situation.
**Structure:** Plain form (drop だ after noun/な-adjective) + なら, + statement
**Examples:**
1. 市役所へ行くなら、午前中がいいですよ。(If you're going to city hall, mornings are good.)
2. 日本語を勉強するなら、毎日練習したほうがいいです。(If you're going to study Japanese, you'd better practice every day.)
3. お金が必要なら、貸しますよ。(If you need money, I'll lend it to you.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- Confusing なら (reacting to an already-mentioned topic, usually followed by advice about that same topic) with たら/と (about the timing of a future event) — なら typically follows the topic being discussed, while たら requires the condition to actually happen first before the result.
- Using なら for a straightforward past sequence of events where たら fits better (なら is not about "after X happened," it's about "given that X is the topic").

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. If you're going to the bank, I'll go with you.
2. If it's convenient for you, let's meet tomorrow.
3. If you're free, please help me.

### Grammar 2 — 〜くなります／〜になります ("to become...")
**Meaning:** Describes a change of state over time.
**Structure:** い-adjective (drop い) + くなります；な-adjective/noun + になります
**Examples:**
1. 最近、忙しくなりました。(I've become busy recently.)
2. 手続きが簡単になりました。(The procedure has become simple.)
3. 彼は医者になりました。(He became a doctor.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- Applying くなります to な-adjectives/nouns (❌簡単くなります instead of 簡単になります) — the connector changes depending on the adjective type.
- Forgetting いい's irregular change: いい becomes よくなります, not the expected ❌いくなります.

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. It has become cold.
2. The city has become lively.
3. I became a teacher. (use になります, not なりたい)

### Grammar 3 — 受身形〜られます (passive form)
**Meaning:** Expresses that the subject receives an action from someone/something else — often implying inconvenience, or used in formal/official statements.
**Structure:** る-verbs: drop る, add られる；う-verbs: change the final u-sound to a-sound + れる (待つ→待たれる)；irregular: する→される, 来る→来られる
**Examples:**
1. 書類は明日渡されます。(The documents will be handed over tomorrow.)
2. 先生に褒められました。(I was praised by the teacher.)
3. 手続きの期限が変更されました。(The application deadline was changed.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- Forgetting to mark the "doer" of the action with に (褒められました needs 先生に, not 先生を) — Chinese speakers used to active-voice-first phrasing often omit or misplace this marker.
- Over-using the passive to describe plain facts where Japanese would actually prefer active voice — Japanese reserves the passive mainly for being *affected* by someone's action (often negatively) or for formal/official announcements, less freely than English uses "was done."

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. This form is filled in by the staff.
2. I was told to bring my ID card.
3. The deadline was changed (extended).

## ④ Reading

先週、市役所へ行きました。手続きのために、いろいろな書類が必要でした。窓口で、まず記入用紙をもらいました。名前や住所を記入して、印鑑を押しました。もし印鑑を忘れたら、サインでもいいと係の人が教えてくれました。それから、郵便局へ行って、小包を送りました。送料と手数料を払って、口座からお金を引き出しました。全部の手続きが終わるまで、思ったより時間がかかりました。次に行くなら、午前中に行ったほうがいいと思います。

**Comprehension questions:**
1. 市役所で何をしましたか。
2. 印鑑を忘れた場合、何をすればいいですか。
3. 郵便局で何をしましたか。

**Answer key:**
1. 記入用紙に名前や住所を記入して、印鑑を押しました（手続きをしました）。
2. サインをすればいいです。
3. 小包を送って、送料と手数料を払いました。

## 🇹🇼 Chinese Notes

- **証（保険証／身分証明書）**: This is the shinjitai form of Traditional Chinese 證. It looks closer to Simplified Chinese 证 than to the 證 Taiwanese learners write by hand — another reminder that Japanese kanji forms sometimes track Simplified Chinese rather than Traditional Chinese.
- **印鑑 vs. 印章**: Taiwan does use personal seals (印章) for some legal matters, but daily life relies much more on handwritten signatures. In Japan, 印鑑 remains far more central to everyday bureaucracy — banks, apartment leases, and even parcel pickup can require a personal seal, which surprises many Chinese-speaking newcomers who expect a signature (サイン) to always be enough.
- **口座 vs. 戶頭/帳戶**: The concept matches Chinese 帳戶 exactly, but note 口座 uses 口 (mouth/opening) rather than any character resembling 戶 or 帳 — don't try to guess this word's kanji from Chinese banking vocabulary; it must be learned as its own compound.
