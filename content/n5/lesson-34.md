### Word-by-word details

**1. 許可（きょか）— 許可**
- 例文: 使用の許可をもらいました。
- 翻譯: 得到了使用的許可。
- Common mistake: Confusing the formal noun 許可 with the everyday verb 許す (Lesson 22, "to forgive/allow") — 許可 is the official, written-register word; 許す is the plain, spoken verb.
- Memory tip: 許（allow）+ 可（permissible）= "an official allowance."

**2. 禁止（きんし）— 禁止**
- 例文: ここは駐車禁止です。
- 翻譯: 這裡禁止停車。
- Common mistake: Confusing the noun/sign-label 禁止 (駐車禁止, "no parking") with the active verb 禁止する (#21 this lesson, "to prohibit [something]") — one is a fixed label, the other is a full verb.
- Memory tip: 禁（forbid）+ 止（stop）= "forbid and stop."

**3. 規則（きそく）— 規則**
- 例文: 学校の規則を守ってください。
- 翻譯: 請遵守學校的規則。
- Common mistake: Treating 規則 and ルール as always interchangeable — 規則 sounds formal/official (school, workplace), while ルール is casual, often used for games.
- Memory tip: Nearly identical to Chinese 規則 in both meaning and characters.

**4. ルール — 規則**
- 例文: このゲームのルールが分かりません。
- 翻譯: 我不懂這個遊戲的規則。
- Common mistake: Using ルール in very formal or legal writing where 規則 would be more appropriate.
- Memory tip: Borrowed directly from English "rule" — easy to recognize by sound.

**5. 守る（まもる）— 遵守、保護**
- 例文: 約束を守ります。
- 翻譯: 遵守約定。
- Common mistake: Using the wrong particle — 守る takes を for the thing being kept/protected (規則を守る), not に.
- Memory tip: Contains 寸 (a small unit) under a roof 宀 — protecting something precious kept safely under a roof.

**6. 注意する（ちゅういする）— 注意、警告**
- 例文: 車に注意してください。
- 翻譯: 請小心車輛。
- Common mistake: Assuming 注意する only means "to be careful" — it can also mean "to warn/scold someone" in a stronger sense (先生に注意されました, "I was warned by the teacher").
- Memory tip: 注（pour/focus）+ 意（attention）= "pouring your attention" onto something.

**7. 危ない（あぶない）— 危險的**
- 例文: この道は夜、危ないです。
- 翻譯: 這條路晚上很危險。
- Common mistake: Confusing the everyday adjective 危ない with the more formal noun 危険 — 危ない is the common spoken word for "dangerous."
- Memory tip: Shouted alone, あぶない！means "Watch out!" — a very common exclamation.

**8. 安全な（あんぜんな）— 安全**
- 例文: この公園は子供にとって安全です。
- 翻譯: 這個公園對小孩來說很安全。
- Common mistake: Treating 安全 as an い-adjective (❌安全いです) — it's a na-adjective and needs です/な before a noun.
- Memory tip: 安（peaceful, from 安い, Lesson 11）+ 全（complete）= "completely at peace."

**9. 自由な（じゆうな）— 自由**
- 例文: 週末は自由な時間があります。
- 翻譯: 週末有自由的時間。
- Common mistake: Confusing 自由 (freedom, liberty of choice) with anything related to price — 自由 never means "free of charge," only freedom/liberty.
- Memory tip: 自（self）+ 由（from）= "acting from oneself" — freedom.

**10. 決まり（きまり）— 規定、習慣**
- 例文: これはこの家の決まりです。
- 翻譯: 這是這個家的規定。
- Common mistake: Confusing the noun 決まり (an established rule/custom) with the verb 決まる (Lesson 37, "to be decided") — related in root meaning, different parts of speech.
- Memory tip: 決まり comes from 決める (Lesson 22, "to decide") — "something that has been decided [as a rule]."

**11. 条件（じょうけん）— 條件**
- 例文: 参加の条件を確認してください。
- 翻譯: 請確認參加的條件。
- Common mistake: Mixing 条件 (a prerequisite/requirement) up with 決まり/規則 (specific established rules) — 条件 refers to a condition that must be satisfied, not a standing rule.
- Memory tip: 条（clause/item）+ 件（matter）= "a clause/item that must be met."

**12. 義務（ぎむ）— 義務**
- 例文: 税金を払うのは国民の義務です。
- 翻譯: 繳稅是國民的義務。
- Common mistake: Confusing the noun 義務 (a formal obligation) with the grammar pattern 〜なければなりません (Lesson 31's way of saying "must") — 義務 names the obligation itself, it isn't a verb pattern.
- Memory tip: Matches Chinese 義務 closely in both form and meaning.

**13. 禁煙（きんえん）— 禁菸**
- 例文: このレストランは禁煙です。
- 翻譯: 這家餐廳禁菸。
- Common mistake: Treating 禁煙 as a verb (❌禁煙します meaning "I forbid smoking") — as a policy/sign word it's a noun stating a state (禁煙です, "[this place] is no-smoking").
- Memory tip: 禁（forbid）+ 煙（smoke）= "forbidden smoke."

**14. 立入禁止（たちいりきんし）— 禁止進入**
- 例文: この先は立入禁止です。
- 翻譯: 前方禁止進入。
- Common mistake: Trying to build this compound from scratch (入る禁止) instead of recognizing 立入禁止 as one fixed sign phrase seen everywhere in Japan.
- Memory tip: 立入（entering, "standing and entering"）+ 禁止（prohibition）= a phrase to recognize instantly on signs.

**15. 必ず（かならず）— 一定、必然**
- 例文: 明日は必ず来てください。
- 翻譯: 明天請一定要來。
- Common mistake: Confusing 必ず (certainty that an action will/must happen) with かなり (Lesson 33, "considerably") — similar sound, unrelated meaning.
- Memory tip: 必（must）+ ず (adverbial ending) = "without fail."

**16. 絶対に（ぜったいに）— 絕對**
- 例文: 絶対に遅刻しないでください。
- 翻譯: 請絕對不要遲到。
- Common mistake: Using 絶対に and 必ず interchangeably — 絶対に carries stronger emphasis/emotion ("absolutely, no matter what"), while 必ず is more neutral.
- Memory tip: Matches Chinese 絕對 closely in both form and meaning.

**17. 勝手に（かってに）— 擅自**
- 例文: 人の物を勝手に使わないでください。
- 翻譯: 請不要擅自使用別人的東西。
- Common mistake: Missing the negative connotation — 勝手に almost always implies acting selfishly/without asking, not a neutral "independently."
- Memory tip: 勝手 suggests doing things "your own way" regardless of others — often disapproving in tone.

**18. 自分で（じぶんで）— 自己**
- 例文: この宿題は自分でしてください。
- 翻譯: 這個作業請自己做。
- Common mistake: Confusing 自分で (doing something oneself, through one's own effort) with 一人で ("alone," a related but distinct idea about being unaccompanied).
- Memory tip: 自分（oneself）+ で（means marker）= "by means of oneself."

**19. 責任（せきにん）— 責任**
- 例文: この仕事は私の責任です。
- 翻譯: 這份工作是我的責任。
- Common mistake: Forgetting 責任 needs です/があります rather than acting as a verb on its own (❌責任します does not exist).
- Memory tip: Matches Chinese 責任 directly in both form and meaning.

**20. 使用する（しようする）— 使用（正式）**
- 例文: このトイレは自由に使用できます。
- 翻譯: 這個廁所可以自由使用。
- Common mistake: Overusing the casual 使う (Lesson 6) in formal signage where 使用する is expected, or the reverse — using 使用する in casual chat where 使う sounds more natural.
- Memory tip: 使用する is the formal, written-register twin of the everyday verb 使う.

**21. 禁止する（きんしする）— 禁止**
- 例文: 学校は携帯電話の使用を禁止しています。
- 翻譯: 學校禁止使用手機。
- Common mistake: Confusing the active verb 禁止する ("to prohibit [something]") with the standalone sign-label noun 禁止 (#2).
- Memory tip: Add する to the noun 禁止 to turn "prohibition" into "to prohibit."

**22. 従う（したがう）— 遵從**
- 例文: 規則に従ってください。
- 翻譯: 請遵從規則。
- Common mistake: Using を instead of に — 従う specifically takes に (規則に従う, not ❌規則を従う).
- Memory tip: 従 shows people following one after another — "to follow along with" a rule or leader.

**23. 違反する（いはんする）— 違反**
- 例文: 交通ルールに違反しました。
- 翻譯: 違反了交通規則。
- Common mistake: Using を instead of に — 違反する also takes に (規則に違反する), matching the same particle pattern as 従う.
- Memory tip: 違（differ, from 違う）+ 反（oppose）= "to go against and deviate from" a rule.

**24. 認める（みとめる）— 承認、認可**
- 例文: 先生は私の意見を認めました。
- 翻譯: 老師認可了我的意見。
- Common mistake: Confusing 認める (to approve/officially acknowledge, or admit something is true) with 分かる (Lesson 23, "to understand") — 認める implies a decision or acceptance, not mere comprehension.
- Memory tip: 認（recognize）+ める = "to formally recognize [as valid/true]."

**25. 制限する（せいげんする）— 限制**
- 例文: 時間を制限します。
- 翻譯: 限制時間。
- Common mistake: Confusing 制限する (to actively impose a partial restriction) with 禁止する (to prohibit entirely) — 制限 implies a limit, not a total ban.
- Memory tip: 制（control）+ 限（limit）= "to control by limiting."

## ② Kanji (4): 使, 守, 自, 立

**使 — Meaning: 使 (use)**
- On-reading: シ
- Kun-reading: つか.う
- Words: 使う（つかう, Lesson 6）, 使用する（しようする, this lesson）
- Example sentence: 箸を使って食べます。
- Stroke order: 8 strokes — write the person radical 亻on the left first, then build the 吏 component on the right (top horizontal, box shape, then the vertical stroke through it).
- Chinese difference: Same shape/meaning as Chinese 使 ("use"; also "envoy" in 使者/大使). For N5 purposes, Japanese 使 mainly appears in the "to use" sense (使う, 使用する) — the "envoy/ambassador" sense also exists (大使) but is much less common at this level.

**守 — Meaning: 守 (protect, keep)**
- On-reading: シュ
- Kun-reading: まも.る
- Words: 守る（まもる, this lesson）
- Example sentence: 規則を守ります。
- Stroke order: 6 strokes — the roof radical 宀 first (dot, then two sides), then 寸 underneath (short horizontal, vertical hook, short dot).
- Chinese difference: Same shape/meaning as Chinese 守 ("guard/protect/keep"), used similarly in both languages for keeping rules and promises (守約 in Chinese, 約束を守る in Japanese) — a smooth, low-risk transfer.

**自 — Meaning: 自 (self)**
- On-reading: ジ、シ
- Kun-reading: みずか.ら
- Words: 自分（じぶん, this lesson）, 自由（じゆう, this lesson）, 自然（しぜん, Lesson 24）
- Example sentence: 自分で決めてください。
- Stroke order: 6 strokes — top short stroke first, then the enclosing box-like shape, finishing with the two internal horizontal strokes.
- Chinese difference: Same shape/meaning as Chinese 自 ("self," as in 自己). Japanese rarely uses 自 as a standalone word — it almost always appears inside a compound like 自分, 自由, or 自然.

**立 — Meaning: 立 (stand)**
- On-reading: リツ
- Kun-reading: た.つ
- Words: 立入禁止（たちいりきんし, this lesson）, 立つ（たつ, to stand）
- Example sentence: ここに立たないでください。
- Stroke order: 5 strokes — top dot first, then the horizontal stroke, finishing with the two legs (left stroke, then right stroke) spreading from the base, like a person standing with feet planted.
- Chinese difference: Same shape/meaning as Chinese 立 ("stand," also "establish," as in 立法). Both languages use it productively in compounds about establishing or standing something up, though 立入禁止 is a distinctly Japanese sign phrase with no direct one-to-one Chinese equivalent.

## ③ Grammar (3 points)

### Grammar 1 — 〜てしまいます (completion, unfortunate result)
**Meaning:** Expresses that an action is completed thoroughly, or that something happened unfortunately/regrettably, often unintentionally.
**Structure:** Verb te-form + しまいます.
**Examples:**
1. 宿題を全部やってしまいました。(I finished all the homework.)
2. 電車の中でかさを忘れてしまいました。(I unfortunately forgot my umbrella on the train.)
3. 財布を落としてしまいました。(I dropped my wallet, unfortunately.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- ❌ Using てしまいました for every past action — this overstates regret/finality where a neutral ました would sound more natural for a plain past event.
- ❌ Mixing the casual contraction ちゃう/ちゃった into otherwise formal です/ます writing.

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. I accidentally dropped my wallet.
2. I finished reading the book.
3. I forgot the rule.

### Grammar 2 — 〜ようになります (to come to be able to / to start)
**Meaning:** Describes a change of state over time — becoming able to do something, or a new habit/situation starting, that wasn't the case before.
**Structure:** Verb potential form (or plain dictionary form for habits) + ようになります.
**Examples:**
1. 漢字が読めるようになりました。(I've come to be able to read kanji.)
2. 毎日野菜を食べるようになりました。(I've started eating vegetables every day.)
3. 日本語で話せるようになりたいです。(I want to become able to speak in Japanese.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- ❌ Confusing 〜ようになります (a gradual change of state over time) with a plain habitual ます statement — ようになります specifically highlights "this is different from before."
- ❌ Forgetting ように is required — attaching なります directly to a plain verb changes the meaning entirely.

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. I've come to be able to use chopsticks.
2. He has started waking up early.
3. I want to become able to speak Japanese well.

### Grammar 3 — 〜ことができます (can do, ability)
**Meaning:** A more formal/explicit way to express ability, "can do X," using a nominalized verb clause.
**Structure:** Verb dictionary form + ことができます.
**Examples:**
1. 私は漢字を読むことができます。(I can read kanji.)
2. この公園で自由に遊ぶことができます。(You can play freely in this park.)
3. 明日は早く来ることができません。(I can't come early tomorrow.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- ❌ Combining ことができます redundantly with the potential form in the same clause (❌読めることができます → choose either 読めます or 読むことができます, not both).
- ❌ Forgetting こと — verbs cannot attach directly to できます (❌読むできます → ⭕読むことができます).

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. You can use this room freely.
2. I cannot come to the meeting tomorrow.
3. Anyone can enter this park.

3. みんなが図書館を使うことができます。(Everyone can use the library.)

## 🇹🇼 Chinese Notes

- **勝手に（かってに）**: The individual characters "勝" (win) and "手" (hand) give a Chinese reader no hint of the meaning — in Japanese, 勝手に means "as one pleases / without asking permission," almost always with a disapproving tone. This is a genuine false friend with no transparent link to the literal characters.
- **使（使う／使用する）**: In Chinese, 使 alone commonly means "to make someone do something" (使他去) or "envoy." In Japanese at the N5 level, 使 is essentially just "to use" (使う, 使用する) — the "make someone do" meaning is expressed by an entirely different grammar pattern (the causative form), not by this kanji.
- **自由（じゆうな）**: Chinese 自由 also means "freedom," but in everyday Japanese, 自由な often specifically describes free/unstructured time or unrestricted choice (自由な時間) rather than political liberty — the political sense exists too, but isn't the first association in everyday contexts like this lesson's park and library rules.
