### Word-by-word details

**1. できる — 能夠、可以**
- 例文: 日本語で電話ができますか。
- 翻譯: 你能夠用日文打電話嗎？
- Common mistake: できる takes が for the thing one is able to do, not を (❌日本語をできます) — a very common transfer error from object-marking habits.
- Memory tip: できる is the "ability" partner of する — think of する→できる the way English turns "do" into "can do."

**2. 話せる — 會說**
- 例文: 彼女は英語が話せます。
- 翻譯: 她會說英文。
- Common mistake: This is the potential form of 話す, not a separate word — learners sometimes always say 話すことができる instead of using the shorter, equally correct 話せる.
- Memory tip: 話す（speak）→ 話せる（potential form）— swap す for せる.

**3. 読める — 能讀**
- 例文: 漢字が少し読めます。
- 翻譯: 我能讀一點漢字。
- Common mistake: Confusing 読める (potential, "can read") with 読む (plain, "to read") when translating "I can read" — the potential form is required.
- Memory tip: 読む→読める follows the same u→e potential shift as 話す→話せる.

**4. 書ける — 能寫**
- 例文: 名前が漢字で書けます。
- 翻譯: 名字能用漢字寫。
- Common mistake: The same u→e pattern trap; also confusing 書ける (potential of 書く) with the plain form 書く itself.
- Memory tip: 書く→書ける — the same u→e shift again.

**5. 聞こえる — 聽得見**
- 例文: 隣の部屋から音楽が聞こえます。
- 翻譯: 從隔壁房間聽得見音樂。
- Common mistake: Confusing 聞こえる (something is audible to the ear, involuntary) with the potential of 聞く ("able to listen/ask," a deliberate act) — 聞こえる describes what naturally reaches your ears.
- Memory tip: こえる resembles 声（こえ, voice）— sound "reaching" your ears.

**6. 見える — 看得見**
- 例文: ここから富士山が見えます。
- 翻譯: 從這裡看得見富士山。
- Common mistake: Confusing 見える (something is visible, involuntary) with the potential of 見る, 見られる ("able to watch/see on purpose").
- Memory tip: Pairs with 聞こえる — both describe natural sensory perception, not deliberate effort.

**7. 上手な（じょうずな）— 擅長的**
- 例文: 田中さんは料理が上手です。
- 翻譯: 田中先生做菜很拿手。
- Common mistake: Chinese speakers may casually praise their own skill, but in Japanese it sounds boastful to describe your own ability with 上手 — use 得意 for yourself instead, and save 上手 for praising others.
- Memory tip: 上（above/high）+ 手（hand）= "skilled hands," rated highly.

**8. 下手な（へたな）— 不擅長的**
- 例文: 私は歌が下手です。
- 翻譯: 我唱歌不太好聽。
- Common mistake: Using 下手 to describe someone else directly to their face can sound rude — it's safest for describing yourself or a neutral third party.
- Memory tip: 下（below/low）+ 手（hand）= the opposite of 上手, "unskilled hands."

**9. 練習する — 練習**
- 例文: 毎日漢字を練習します。
- 翻譯: 每天練習漢字。
- Common mistake: Confusing 練習する (to practice, repeated training toward a skill) with 勉強する (Lesson 6, to study in general) — they overlap but 練習 implies repetition to build a specific skill.
- Memory tip: 練（train/refine）+ 習（learn, from 習う, Lesson 22）= "training through repeated learning."

**10. 教わる（おそわる）— 受教、學習**
- 例文: 先生に日本語を教わりました。
- 翻譯: 向老師學了日文。
- Common mistake: Confusing 教わる (to be taught by someone, "receive teaching") with 教える (Lesson 22, "to teach someone") — the direction of the action is reversed.
- Memory tip: Same kanji as 教える, but the わる ending gives it a "receiving" feel — "to receive teaching."

**11. 続ける（つづける）— 繼續**
- 例文: 日本語の勉強を続けます。
- 翻譯: 繼續學習日文。
- Common mistake: Confusing the transitive 続ける ("to continue [something]") with the intransitive 続く ("[something] continues") — 勉強を続ける vs 勉強が続く.
- Memory tip: 続 contains 糸（thread）— picture a thread that keeps going, unbroken.

**12. 止める（やめる）— 停止、戒掉**
- 例文: たばこを止めるつもりです。
- 翻譯: 我打算戒菸。
- Common mistake: This is read exactly like 辞める (Lesson 28, "to quit a job") — same pronunciation, different kanji: 止める is general "stop/quit any habit," while 辞める specifically means resigning from a position.
- Memory tip: 止（stop）+ める = "to make [something] stop."

**13. 始める（はじめる）— 開始**
- 例文: 来月から新しい仕事を始めます。
- 翻譯: 下個月開始新工作。
- Common mistake: Confusing the transitive 始める ("to start [something]") with the intransitive 始まる (Lesson 16, "[something] starts") — 授業を始める vs 授業が始まる.
- Memory tip: Pairs with 続ける — memorize 始める・始まる as a transitive/intransitive pair, just like 続ける・続く.

**14. 能力（のうりょく）— 能力**
- 例文: 彼は語学の能力が高いです。
- 翻譯: 他的語言能力很強。
- Common mistake: Saying ❌能力が上手 by blending grammar patterns — 能力 pairs with 高い／低い ("high/low"), not 上手／下手, which describe the person, not the ability itself.
- Memory tip: 能（able）+ 力（power）= "the power to be able to [do something]."

**15. 才能（さいのう）— 才能**
- 例文: 彼女には音楽の才能があります。
- 翻譯: 她有音樂才能。
- Common mistake: Confusing 才能 (innate talent) with 能力 (ability, which can be trained) — 才能があります describes a natural gift, not a learned skill.
- Memory tip: 才（talent, as in 天才, genius）+ 能（able）= "talented ability."

**16. 可能（かのう）— 可能**
- 例文: これは可能ですか。
- 翻譯: 這是可能的嗎？
- Common mistake: Using 可能 as a verb directly (❌可能します) — it's a na-adjective/noun, so it pairs with です/ではありません rather than attaching directly to an action.
- Memory tip: Same characters and meaning as Chinese 可能 — an easy direct match.

**17. 不可能（ふかのう）— 不可能**
- 例文: 一日で終わるのは不可能です。
- 翻譯: 一天內完成是不可能的。
- Common mistake: None major — the 不 negation prefix works exactly like Chinese 不, making this one of the easiest words in the lesson.
- Memory tip: 不（not）+ 可能（possible）= the direct opposite, just like in Chinese.

**18. マスターする — 精通、掌握**
- 例文: 一年で日本語をマスターしたいです。
- 翻譯: 我想在一年內精通日文。
- Common mistake: Overusing this casual loanword in very formal writing; for N5 purposes it's perfectly natural in everyday speech.
- Memory tip: Borrowed directly from English "master" + する — easy to recognize by sound.

**19. 身につける（みにつける）— 掌握（技能）**
- 例文: 新しい技術を身につけました。
- 翻譯: 掌握了新技能。
- Common mistake: Confusing this idiom with literally "wearing" something — 身につける here is figurative, meaning to internalize a skill, even though the same phrase can also literally mean "to put on" clothing or accessories.
- Memory tip: 身（one's body/self）+ につける（attach to）= "to attach [a skill] to yourself."

**20. 挑戦する（ちょうせんする）— 挑戰**
- 例文: 新しいことに挑戦したいです。
- 翻譯: 我想挑戰新的事物。
- Common mistake: Assuming this means "to challenge someone to a fight" — 挑戦する usually describes taking on a difficult task yourself, not confronting another person.
- Memory tip: 挑（provoke）+ 戦（battle）= "taking on a battle," usually a personal one.

**21. 成長する（せいちょうする）— 成長**
- 例文: 子供はすぐに成長します。
- 翻譯: 小孩子很快就長大了。
- Common mistake: Assuming 成長する only applies to physical growth — it's also used for abstract growth (a company, one's skills), where learners sometimes default only to 大きくなる instead.
- Memory tip: 成（become）+ 長（grow）= "to become grown."

**22. 達成する（たっせいする）— 達成**
- 例文: 目標を達成しました。
- 翻譯: 達成了目標。
- Common mistake: Confusing 達成する (to achieve a goal) with completing a task in the sense of finishing a product — 達成 specifically pairs with 目標 (goal).
- Memory tip: 達（reach/arrive）+ 成（accomplish）= "to arrive at completion."

**23. 諦める（あきらめる）— 放棄**
- 例文: 難しくても諦めないでください。
- 翻譯: 就算很難也請不要放棄。
- Common mistake: Confusing 諦める (to give up on a goal/hope) with 止める (#12, to stop an action/habit) — 諦める carries emotional weight about abandoning hope, not simply stopping physically.
- Memory tip: Contains 帝（emperor）— even an emperor sometimes has to "give up" and accept reality.

**24. 努力する（どりょくする）— 努力**
- 例文: 合格するために努力します。
- 翻譯: 為了合格而努力。
- Common mistake: None major — 努力 matches Chinese 努力 closely in both meaning and characters, though Japanese always needs する attached to use it as a verb.
- Memory tip: Nearly identical to Chinese 努力 in both form and meaning — an easy transfer word.

**25. 集中する（しゅうちゅうする）— 集中**
- 例文: 勉強に集中したいです。
- 翻譯: 我想專心讀書。
- Common mistake: Translating "concentrate" using 頑張る (Lesson 16, "to do one's best") instead of 集中する, which specifically means to focus attention rather than to try hard in general.
- Memory tip: 集（gather）+ 中（middle）= "gathering [your attention] into the center."

## ② Kanji (2): 上, 下

**上 — Meaning: 上 (up, above)**
- On-reading: ジョウ
- Kun-reading: うえ、あ.げる
- Words: 上（うえ, above/on top）, 上手（じょうず, skillful — this lesson）, 目上（めうえ, one's senior/superior）
- Example sentence: 本はテーブルの上にあります。
- Stroke order: 3 strokes — short vertical stroke first, then the long horizontal base stroke, finishing with the short stroke above it.
- Chinese difference: Same shape and meaning as Chinese 上, used the same way for physical "above" and abstract superiority (上司, boss). One trap: 上手（じょうず, skillful) is a fixed idiomatic word, not a literal "upper hand" as a word-for-word Chinese reading might suggest.

**下 — Meaning: 下 (down, below)**
- On-reading: カ、ゲ
- Kun-reading: した、さ.げる
- Words: 下（した, below）, 下手（へた, unskillful — this lesson）, 地下（ちか, underground）
- Example sentence: 猫は机の下にいます。
- Stroke order: 3 strokes — short vertical stroke first, then the long horizontal base stroke, finishing with the short stroke below it (a mirror of 上).
- Chinese difference: Same shape and meaning as Chinese 下. Same trap as 上手: 下手（へた, unskillful) is a fixed idiomatic pair with 上手 and does not literally mean "lower hand."

## ③ Grammar (3 points)

### Grammar 1 — 〜つもりです (intend to)
**Meaning:** Expresses a settled intention or plan to do (or not do) something.
**Structure:** Verb dictionary form + つもりです (plan to do); Verb nai-form + つもりです (plan not to do).
**Examples:**
1. 日本語を毎日勉強するつもりです。(I intend to study Japanese every day.)
2. 今年は旅行に行かないつもりです。(I don't intend to travel this year.)
3. 来月からピアノを習うつもりです。(I intend to start learning piano next month.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- ❌ Confusing つもり (a decided, settled intention) with 〜たいです (Lesson 19, a wish/desire that may not be firm) — つもり implies you have already decided, not just that you'd like to.
- ❌ Attaching つもりです to a ました-form verb instead of the dictionary/nai form (❌勉強しましたつもりです → ⭕勉強するつもりです).

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. I intend to practice every day.
2. I don't intend to give up.
3. She intends to master Japanese in one year.

### Grammar 2 — 〜でしょうか（丁寧な質問）
**Meaning:** A softer, more polite way to ask a question than 〜ですか, often used with strangers, customers, or in formal situations.
**Structure:** Sentence (plain form, or noun/adjective) + でしょうか.
**Examples:**
1. これは可能でしょうか。(Would this be possible?)
2. すみません、今、お時間はよろしいでしょうか。(Excuse me, do you have a moment?)
3. どちらが上手でしょうか。(I wonder which one is more skillful?)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- ❌ Treating でしょうか as identical to ですか in every context — でしょうか is reserved for extra politeness or genuine uncertainty, and sounds oddly stiff in casual chats with friends.

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. Would this be possible?
2. I wonder if he can speak English.
3. Excuse me, is now a good time?

### Grammar 3 — 〜すぎます (too much)
**Meaning:** Expresses excess — "too [adjective]" or "[verb] too much."
**Structure:** い-adjective stem (drop い) + すぎます; な-adjective stem + すぎます; Verb ます-stem + すぎます.
**Examples:**
1. この漢字は難しすぎます。(This kanji is too difficult.)
2. 昨日は勉強しすぎました。(I studied too much yesterday.)
3. 彼は話しすぎます。(He talks too much.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- ❌ Attaching すぎます to the full adjective instead of its stem (❌難しいすぎます → ⭕難しすぎます — the い must be dropped).
- ❌ Inserting の before すぎます with a na-adjective (❌上手のすぎます → ⭕上手すぎます — no の needed).

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. This is too expensive.
2. I practiced too much.
3. This test is too difficult.

## ④ Reading

Read the passage aloud:

私は今、日本語を勉強しています。日本語は上手ではありませんが、漢字が少し読めます。毎日書く練習をしています。来年も日本語を練習するつもりです。難しいですが、諦めません。日本語をマスターしたいです。

**Comprehension questions:**
1. 今、何を勉強していますか。(What is being studied right now?)
2. 漢字はどうですか、読めますか。(How are the kanji — can they be read?)
3. 来年、何をするつもりですか。(What is the plan for next year?)

**Answer key:**
1. 日本語を勉強しています。(Japanese is being studied.)
2. はい、少し読めます。(Yes, a little can be read.)
3. 日本語を練習するつもりです。(The plan is to keep practicing Japanese.)

## 🇹🇼 Chinese Notes

- **上手／下手（じょうず／へた）**: These idiomatic words don't literally mean "upper hand/lower hand" the way a word-for-word Chinese reading might suggest — they mean "skillful/unskillful." Japanese etiquette also considers it slightly boastful to praise your own skill with 上手; use 得意 when talking about yourself instead.
- **止める vs. 辞める**: Both are read やめる and both broadly mean "to quit," but they use different kanji for different scopes — 止める is a general "stop/quit any habit," while 辞める (Lesson 28) specifically means resigning from a job. This is a pure-reading trap that's invisible in speech but visible in writing.
- **才能 vs. 能力**: Chinese speakers may treat these as interchangeable, but 才能 specifically means innate, natural talent, while 能力 means a trainable ability — mixing them up changes the nuance of a compliment.
