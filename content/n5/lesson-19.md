### Vocabulary in Depth

**1. 電話（でんわ）— telephone**
- Example: 電話をかけます。
- Translation: 我要打電話。
- Common mistake: Using を with connects fine, but learners often use する instead of かける for "to make a call" — 電話をする is also acceptable, but 電話をかける is the classic textbook phrase; don't confuse either with 電話に出る (to answer a call).
- Memory tip: 電（electric）+ 話（talk）= "electric talking," i.e., the telephone.

**2. 携帯（けーたい）— cell phone**
- Example: 携帯を忘れました。
- Translation: 我忘了帶手機。
- Common mistake: Assuming 携帯 always needs 電話 attached — 携帯 alone (short for 携帯電話) is the standard everyday word for "cell phone"; adding 電話 (携帯電話) is more formal/complete but not required in casual speech.
- Memory tip: In Chinese, 携帶 is a verb meaning "to carry"; in Japanese, 携帯 by itself has narrowed to mean "cell phone" as a noun — see Chinese Notes below.

**3. スマホ — smartphone**
- Example: 新しいスマホがほしいです。
- Translation: 我想要一支新的智慧型手機。
- Common mistake: Treating スマホ and 携帯 as identical — スマホ specifically means a smartphone, while 携帯 can still refer to older non-smart "feature phones" for some speakers, though today the two overlap heavily.
- Memory tip: A shortened form of スマートフォン ("smartphone") — Japanese loves clipping long katakana words (compare パソコン below).

**4. メール — email**
- Example: 友達にメールを送りました。
- Translation: 我寄了電子郵件給朋友。
- Common mistake: Using 手紙 (a physical, handwritten/mailed letter) where メール (email) is meant — the two are not interchangeable even though both mean "written correspondence."
- Memory tip: From English "mail" — but note it specifically means email in modern Japanese, not physical postal mail.

**5. 手紙（てがみ）— letter**
- Example: おばあさんに手紙を書きました。
- Translation: 我寫了一封信給奶奶。
- Common mistake: This is a major false friend for Chinese speakers — see Chinese Notes below; do not confuse 手紙 (letter) with its Chinese homograph 手紙 (toilet paper)!
- Memory tip: 手（hand）+ 紙（paper）= "paper [written by] hand" — a handwritten letter.

**6. インターネット — internet**
- Example: インターネットで調べます。
- Translation: 用網路查資料。
- Common mistake: Shortening it incorrectly — some learners try ❌ インター alone, but the common casual shortening in Japanese is actually ネット, not インター.
- Memory tip: From English "internet" — in-ta-a-ne-t-to; the common short form is just ネット.

**7. パソコン — computer**
- Example: パソコンでメールを書きます。
- Translation: 用電腦寫電子郵件。
- Common mistake: Using コンピューター (a longer, more formal loanword) and パソコン interchangeably without realizing パソコン specifically implies a personal computer (from "personal computer"), while コンピューター is the broader general term.
- Memory tip: Clipped from パーソナルコンピューター ("personal computer") — another classic Japanese abbreviation, just like スマホ.

**8. アプリ — app**
- Example: このアプリは便利です。
- Translation: 這個App很方便。
- Common mistake: Confusing アプリ (a mobile/software application) with ソフト (a broader term for "software," often for desktop programs) — アプリ leans toward phone/tablet apps in everyday use.
- Memory tip: Clipped from アプリケーション ("application") — yet another shortened katakana word.

**9. 送る（おくる）— to send**
- Example: 写真を送ってください。
- Translation: 請傳照片給我。
- Common mistake: Confusing 送る（おくる, to send something）with 贈る（おくる, to present/give a gift, same pronunciation but a different, more formal kanji) — for everyday sending of messages/photos, 送る is what you want.
- Memory tip: Pair with もらう below: 送る (send) → もらう (receive), a natural give/receive pair.

**10. かける — to make a call**
- Example: 母に電話をかけます。
- Translation: 我要打電話給媽媽。
- Common mistake: Using に with the wrong noun — the person you call takes に: 母に電話をかける, not ❌ 母を電話をかける.
- Memory tip: かける has many meanings across Japanese (to hang, to wear glasses, to spend time) — for this lesson, anchor it specifically to the fixed phrase 電話をかける.

**11. もらう — to receive**
- Example: 友達からプレゼントをもらいました。
- Translation: 我收到朋友的禮物。
- Common mistake: Using に instead of から to mark who you received something from — もらう typically uses から (or に) for the giver: 友達からもらう, most commonly with から.
- Memory tip: Part of the あげる／もらう／くれる trio (this lesson, #12–13) — the three verbs of Japanese's direction-sensitive giving-and-receiving system.

**12. あげる — to give**
- Example: 友達にプレゼントをあげます。
- Translation: 我要送禮物給朋友。
- Common mistake: Using あげる when the recipient is "me" (the speaker) — あげる is used when someone other than the speaker gives to someone else (or the speaker gives to someone else); when someone gives TO the speaker, くれる is used instead (see #13).
- Memory tip: Direction: あげる points "outward," away from the speaker toward someone else.

**13. くれる — to give (to me)**
- Example: 友達が私にプレゼントをくれました。
- Translation: 朋友送禮物給我。
- Common mistake: Using あげる instead of くれる when someone gives something to the speaker — this is one of the most distinctive N5 grammar traps for Chinese speakers, since Chinese 給 doesn't change form based on direction the way Japanese does.
- Memory tip: Direction: くれる points "inward," toward the speaker — remember あげる (away from me) vs. くれる (toward me) as a directional pair.

**14. 連絡する（れんらくする）— to contact**
- Example: 後で連絡します。
- Translation: 我等一下會聯絡你。
- Common mistake: Confusing 連絡する (a general term for "to get in touch," by any method) with 電話する (specifically "to call") — 連絡する is broader and doesn't specify the method (call, text, email).
- Memory tip: 連（connect/link）+ 絡（entangle/tie）= "linking [two people] together."

**15. 検索する（けんさくする）— to search**
- Example: インターネットで検索します。
- Translation: 在網路上搜尋。
- Common mistake: Using 探す（さがす, "to look for" a physical/lost item) where 検索する（specifically searching data/information, e.g. online) is meant.
- Memory tip: 検（inspect/check）+ 索（seek/rope）= "checking [while] seeking," a formal word for searching.

**16. 撮る（とる）— to take (a photo)**
- Example: 写真を撮りましょう。
- Translation: 我們拍照吧。
- Common mistake: Confusing 撮る（とる, specifically "to take a photo/video"）with the more general 取る（とる, "to take/pick up" an object) — same pronunciation, different kanji and scope.
- Memory tip: The kanji 撮 contains 手（hand, as the radical 扌）+ 最 (most) — think of "putting your hand to the most important moment."

**17. 壊れる（こわれる）— to break**
- Example: スマホの画面が壊れました。
- Translation: 手機螢幕破掉了。
- Common mistake: Confusing intransitive 壊れる ("[something] breaks by itself/gets broken") with transitive 壊す ("[someone] breaks something") — for describing something being broken/malfunctioning, 壊れる is what you want: 画面が壊れました, not ❌ 画面を壊れました.
- Memory tip: Pair conceptually with 始まる／終わる (Lesson 16) — all are intransitive verbs describing something happening to the subject.

**18. 充電する（じゅうでんする）— to charge (a battery)**
- Example: スマホを充電します。
- Translation: 幫手機充電。
- Common mistake: Using 電気をつける (to turn on a light/power) where 充電する (specifically to charge a battery) is meant — different concepts even though both involve 電 (electricity).
- Memory tip: 充（fill/fulfill）+ 電（electricity）= "filling up with electricity."

**19. 繋がる（つながる）— to connect**
- Example: インターネットに繋がりません。
- Translation: 連不上網路。
- Common mistake: Confusing intransitive 繋がる ("[a connection] forms/exists") with transitive 繋ぐ ("[someone] connects something") — for describing whether Wi-Fi/a call is connecting, 繋がる is the natural choice.
- Memory tip: The kanji 繋 has 糸 (thread) at the bottom — picture a thread linking two things together, like a network connection.

**20. 画面（がめん）— screen**
- Example: 画面が小さいです。
- Translation: 螢幕很小。
- Common mistake: Confusing 画面（がめん, a screen/display）with 写真（しゃしん, Lesson 14, a photo) — 画面 is the device's display surface, not an image itself.
- Memory tip: 画（picture/drawing）+ 面（surface）= "the picture surface," i.e. a screen.

**21. ボタン — button**
- Example: このボタンを押してください。
- Translation: 請按這個按鈕。
- Common mistake: Using 指（finger)-related verbs incorrectly — the standard verb paired with ボタン is 押す (to push), not 引く (to pull), even for small buttons.
- Memory tip: From English "button" — bo-ta-n.

**22. 文字（もじ）— character, text**
- Example: この文字は難しいです。
- Translation: 這個字很難。
- Common mistake: Confusing 文字（もじ, a written character/text symbol）with メール (the message medium itself) — 文字 refers to the characters/text content, not the app or message as a whole.
- Memory tip: 文（writing/sentence）+ 字（character）= "written characters."

**23. 届く（とどく）— to arrive, be delivered**
- Example: 荷物がまだ届きません。
- Translation: 包裹還沒送到。
- Common mistake: Confusing intransitive 届く ("[something] arrives/gets delivered") with transitive 届ける ("[someone] delivers something") — for saying a package/message has arrived, 届く is correct: 荷物が届きました.
- Memory tip: Often paired with 送る in a cause-and-effect pattern: 送ったら、届きます (once you send it, it arrives).

**24. 動画（どうが）— video**
- Example: 動画を見るのが好きです。
- Translation: 我喜歡看影片。
- Common mistake: Confusing 動画（どうが, a video/moving picture）with 映画（えいが, Lesson 14, a movie/film) — similar-sounding, but 動画 is a general/short online video, 映画 is a full theatrical film.
- Memory tip: 動（move）+ 画（picture）= "a moving picture," as opposed to 写真 (a still photo).

**25. 更新する（こうしんする）— to update**
- Example: アプリを更新してください。
- Translation: 請更新這個App。
- Common mistake: Using 新しくする (a more roundabout literal phrasing, "to make new") instead of the standard, more natural 更新する for software/information updates.
- Memory tip: 更（further/again）+ 新（new）= "making [something] new again," i.e., updating.

## ② Kanji (3): 話, 書, 読

**話（talk, speak）**
- Meaning (Chinese): 話
- On-reading: ワ
- Kun-reading: はな.す
- Words: 話す（はなす, Lesson 6）, 電話（でんわ）, 会話（かいわ, conversation）
- Example sentence: 電話で友達と話します。
- Stroke order tip: Write the 言 (speech) radical on the left first (top to bottom, 7 strokes), then 舌 (tongue) on the right — think "words from the tongue."
- Chinese difference: Identical in shape and meaning to Chinese 話; the kun-reading はな.す for the verb "to speak" is a Japanese-only addition layered on top of the shared kanji meaning.

**書（write）**
- Meaning (Chinese): 書
- On-reading: ショ
- Kun-reading: か.く
- Words: 書く（かく, Lesson 6）, 図書館（としょかん, Lesson 7）, 辞書（じしょ, dictionary）
- Example sentence: 手紙を書きます。
- Stroke order tip: Build the top portion (聿-like strokes) first, then finish with 日 at the bottom — 10 strokes total.
- Chinese difference: Same shape and meaning as Chinese 書 (traditional); note the kun-reading か.く is used for the common verb "to write," while the on-reading ショ appears in noun compounds like 図書館 and 辞書.

**読（read）**
- Meaning (Chinese): 讀
- On-reading: ドク
- Kun-reading: よ.む
- Words: 読む（よむ, Lesson 6）, 読書（どくしょ, reading, from 読 + 書）, 音読（おんどく, reading aloud）
- Example sentence: 本を読みます。
- Stroke order tip: Write the 言 (speech) radical on the left first, then 売-like component on the right — 14 strokes; note this is the simplified Japanese form compared to the fuller traditional 讀 used in Chinese.
- Chinese difference: 読 is Japan's simplified form of the traditional Chinese 讀; the right-hand component differs from Taiwan's 讀, so Taiwanese learners should treat 読 as a distinct shape to learn, not assume it looks exactly like the traditional character they already know.

**Kanji Review:** Can you read these? 話す／書く／読む／図書館

## ③ Grammar

### Grammar 1 — 動詞のたい形 (want to do — verb たい form)

**Meaning/Structure:** To say you want to perform an action, attach たい to the ます-stem of a verb (the part before ます) and conjugate it like an い-adjective.
Structure: [Verb ます-stem] + たいです。

**Examples:**
- 新しいスマホが買いたいです。(I want to buy a new smartphone.)
- 友達に電話をかけたいです。(I want to call my friend.)
- 動画を見たいです。(I want to watch a video.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- ❌ 買うたいです → ⭕ 買いたいです — たい attaches to the ます-stem (買い), not the dictionary form (買う); this stem-formation error is very common for beginners.
- Confusing 〜たいです (want to DO something, attaches to a verb) with 〜がほしいです (want a THING, attaches to a noun, Lesson 18) — たい never attaches directly to a noun.
- Note: with たい-form, the object often takes が instead of を (スマホが買いたい), though を is also heard, especially in longer sentences — either is acceptable at N5 level.

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. I want to buy a new phone.
2. I want to send an email.
3. I want to take a photo.
4. I want to search on the internet.

### Grammar 2 — 〜ましょう (let's do...)

**Meaning/Structure:** To suggest doing something together with the listener, attach ましょう to the ます-stem of a verb.
Structure: [Verb ます-stem] + ましょう。

**Examples:**
- 一緒に写真を撮りましょう。(Let's take a photo together.)
- 少し休みましょう。(Let's rest a little.)
- 明日、電話しましょう。(Let's talk on the phone tomorrow.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- Confusing 〜ましょう ("let's," including the speaker) with 〜てください (a request directed at the listener alone) — ましょう implies "we do this together," while てください implies "you do this [for me/because I asked]."
- Confusing 〜ましょう with 〜ませんか (Lesson 16, a softer invitation "won't you...?") — ませんか politely leaves room for the other person to decline, while ましょう is a more direct, assumed-agreement suggestion.

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. Let's send the photo.
2. Let's charge the phone.
3. Let's search on the internet.
4. Let's watch a video together.

## ④ Reading

私は毎日スマホを使います。友達にメールを送って、写真を撮ります。今日、スマホの画面が壊れました。大切な写真を先にパソコンに送りました。それから、母に電話をかけて、新しいスマホがほしいと言いました。母は「じゃあ、一緒に店に行きましょう」と言ってくれました。週末、新しいスマホを買いたいです。

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. 今日、スマホはどうなりましたか。
2. 母は何と言いましたか。
3. 週末、何をしたいですか。

**Answer Key:**
1. 画面が壊れました。(The screen broke.)
2. 「一緒に店に行きましょう」と言いました。("Let's go to the shop together," she said.)
3. 新しいスマホを買いたいです。(They want to buy a new smartphone.)

## ⑤ Listening

*Read once slowly, then once at natural speed.*

A: もしもし、田中です。今、電話大丈夫ですか。
B: はい、大丈夫です。どうしましたか。
A: 昨日の写真、もう送りましたか。
B: あ、まだです。今からメールで送ります。
A: ありがとうございます。それから、新しいアプリを使いたいんですが、教えてもらえますか。
B: いいですよ。週末、一緒に見ましょう。
A: ありがとうございます。よろしくお願いします。

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. Bさんは写真をもう送りましたか。
2. 二人は週末、何をしますか。

**Answer Key:**
1. いいえ、まだです。(No, not yet.)
2. 一緒に新しいアプリを見ます。(They will look at the new app together.)

## 🇹🇼 Chinese Notes

- **手紙 is a major false friend**: In Japanese, 手紙（てがみ）means "letter" — a piece of handwritten correspondence. In Chinese, 手紙 means "toilet paper." Never use this word casually without knowing the context; the meanings could not be more different.
- **携帯 narrows its meaning in Japanese**: In Chinese, 攜帶 is a verb meaning "to carry/bring along." In Japanese, 携帯 used alone as a noun has narrowed to mean specifically "cell phone" (short for 携帯電話), not the general act of carrying something.
