### Word-by-word details

**1. もっと — 更**
- 例文: もっと大きい声で話してください。
- 翻譯: 請說話大聲一點。
- Common mistake: Confusing もっと (comparative "more") with 一番 (superlative "the most") — もっと always compares against a current state, not against a whole group.
- Memory tip: もっと sounds like "more, more!" — an intensifier pushing for extra.

**2. 一番（いちばん）— 最**
- 例文: この店のラーメンが一番おいしいです。
- 翻譯: 這家店的拉麵最好吃。
- Common mistake: Forgetting the fuller structure from Lesson 18, 〜の中で一番〜 ("of all X, the most Y") — 一番 alone in casual speech is fine, but formal writing often needs the comparison group spelled out.
- Memory tip: 一（one）+ 番（rank）= "ranked number one."

**3. より — 比**
- 例文: 電車はバスより速いです。
- 翻譯: 電車比公車快。
- Common mistake: Word order — より attaches right after the thing being compared against (X は Y より Z), so learners sometimes place it incorrectly when translating quickly from Chinese "比".
- Memory tip: Think of より as marking the "starting point" you're measuring away from.

**4. ほう — （比較中的）方**
- 例文: 私は魚より肉のほうが好きです。
- 翻譯: 比起魚，我更喜歡肉。
- Common mistake: Omitting の before ほう (❌肉ほうが好き → ⭕肉のほうが好き) — の is required whenever ほう follows a noun.
- Memory tip: ほう（方）literally means "direction/side" — you're pointing to "the side" that wins the comparison.

**5. 違う（ちがう）— 不同、錯**
- 例文: この二つの答えは違います。
- 翻譯: 這兩個答案不一樣。
- Common mistake: Treating 違う like an い-adjective because it's often translated as "different" — it's actually a u-verb and conjugates like 会う, 買う (違います, not ❌違いです).
- Memory tip: 違う is a verb, not an adjective — always conjugate it like a regular u-verb.

**6. 比べる（くらべる）— 比較**
- 例文: 二つの値段を比べてみます。
- 翻譯: 比較看看這兩個價格。
- Common mistake: Confusing 比べる (the deliberate action of comparing two things) with 違う (the resulting state of being different) — one is an action, the other is a state.
- Memory tip: 比（compare, as in 比較）+ べる = "to put things side by side and compare."

**7. どちらが — 哪一個（禮貌）**
- 例文: コーヒーと紅茶と、どちらが好きですか。
- 翻譯: 咖啡和紅茶，你比較喜歡哪一個？
- Common mistake: Assuming どちら only asks about people (confusing it with あなた) — it actually asks "which one" between two items or options, and is simply more polite than どっち.
- Memory tip: どちら pairs with こちら／そちら／あちら, the polite cousins of これ／それ／あれ (Lesson 3).

**8. どっち — 哪一個（口語）**
- 例文: どっちがいい？
- 翻譯: 哪一個比較好？
- Common mistake: Using どっち in formal writing or with a superior — it's casual/spoken only; どちら is the safe choice in polite contexts.
- Memory tip: どっち is just the shorter, punchier, casual version of どちら.

**9. 長い（ながい）— 長**
- 例文: この川はとても長いです。
- 翻譯: 這條河非常長。
- Common mistake: Confusing the adjective 長い (long, describing physical length or duration) with the suffix 長 in job titles (部長, department manager, Lesson 28) — same kanji, different grammatical role.
- Memory tip: Picture a long line stretching out — 長い literally describes length or duration.

**10. 短い（みじかい）— 短**
- 例文: 今日の会議は短かったです。
- 翻譯: 今天的會議很短。
- Common mistake: Misconjugating the past tense — 短い→短かった, not ❌短いだった.
- Memory tip: 短 pairs directly with 長 as opposites — memorize them together.

**11. 重い（おもい）— 重**
- 例文: このかばんはとても重いです。
- 翻譯: 這個包包非常重。
- Common mistake: Confusing 重い (heavy, physical weight) with the unrelated word 重要な ("important") — they share the kanji 重 but describe different things.
- Memory tip: 重 stacks several horizontal strokes — like stacked weight pressing down.

**12. 軽い（かるい）— 輕**
- 例文: このノートパソコンは軽いです。
- 翻譯: 這台筆電很輕。
- Common mistake: Using 軽い to mean "easy," influenced by Chinese 輕鬆 — in Japanese 軽い is strictly about physical weight; "easy" is 簡単な (Lesson 16).
- Memory tip: Pairs with 重い as its direct opposite — memorize as a pair.

**13. 強い（つよい）— 強**
- 例文: 彼はとても強いです。
- 翻譯: 他非常強壯。
- Common mistake: Overextending 強い to describe strong flavors where a different word might sound more natural in advanced Japanese — for N5 purposes, 強い safely covers most general "strong" meanings.
- Memory tip: Contains 弓（bow）— like a strong bow ready to shoot.

**14. 弱い（よわい）— 弱**
- 例文: 私はお酒に弱いです。
- 翻譯: 我不太能喝酒（酒量差）。
- Common mistake: Translating "weak" too literally in every context — 〜に弱い is an idiom meaning "bad at handling X" (お酒に弱い, can't hold alcohol well), not necessarily physical weakness.
- Memory tip: Pairs with 強い as its direct opposite.

**15. 差（さ）— 差、差距**
- 例文: 二人の身長の差は十センチです。
- 翻譯: 兩人的身高差是十公分。
- Common mistake: Using 差 for a vague, general "difference" — 差 is specifically used for a measurable, numeric gap (身長の差, price differences), not general dissimilarity.
- Memory tip: 差 looks like it has a "cut" through it — the measurable gap between two values.

**16. 以上（いじょう）— 以上**
- 例文: 二十歳以上の人だけ入れます。
- 翻譯: 只有二十歲以上的人可以進入。
- Common mistake: Assuming 以上 excludes the stated number — 二十歳以上 actually includes 20 itself ("20 and above"), not "over 20."
- Memory tip: 以（from this point）+ 上（above）= "from this point upward, inclusive."

**17. 以下（いか）— 以下**
- 例文: 十歳以下の子供は無料です。
- 翻譯: 十歲以下的兒童免費。
- Common mistake: Same inclusive/exclusive trap as 以上 — 十歳以下 includes 10 itself ("10 and below").
- Memory tip: Pairs with 以上 as its mirror opposite — both include the stated number.

**18. 同じくらい（おなじくらい）— 差不多**
- 例文: 兄と弟は身長が同じくらいです。
- 翻譯: 哥哥和弟弟身高差不多。
- Common mistake: Dropping くらい and saying only 同じ when an approximate, casual comparison ("about the same") rather than an exact match is meant.
- Memory tip: 同じ（same, Lesson 2）+ くらい（approximately）= "roughly the same."

**19. まし — 比較好（兩害相權取其輕）**
- 例文: 雨より雪のほうがましです。
- 翻譯: 比起下雨，下雪還算好一點。
- Common mistake: Reading まし as a strongly positive "better" — it actually carries a resigned, lukewarm "less bad" nuance when choosing between two undesirable options.
- Memory tip: Think "if I had to choose, this one's the lesser evil."

**20. かなり — 相當**
- 例文: この問題はかなり難しいです。
- 翻譯: 這個問題相當困難。
- Common mistake: Treating かなり as identical to とても ("very") — かなり is a more objective "considerably," often implying "more than expected."
- Memory tip: Sounds deliberate and measured — like giving a careful, considered assessment.

**21. 少し（すこし）— 一點點**
- 例文: 少し待ってください。
- 翻譯: 請稍等一下。
- Common mistake: Confusing the adverb/noun 少し ("a little," an amount) with the adjective 少ない ("few," describing countable quantity) — different parts of speech that share a kanji.
- Memory tip: Shares the kanji 少 with 少ない — both point to "smallness in amount."

**22. たくさん — 很多**
- 例文: 今日はたくさん勉強しました。
- 翻譯: 今天讀了很多書。
- Common mistake: Forgetting の is needed when たくさん directly modifies a following noun (たくさんの人, "many people"), even though no の is needed before a verb (たくさん勉強する).
- Memory tip: たくさん sounds abundant and full — easy to associate with "lots."

**23. 全然（ぜんぜん）— 完全不**
- 例文: この漢字は全然分かりません。
- 翻譯: 這個漢字我完全不懂。
- Common mistake: Pairing 全然 with a positive statement (全然大丈夫です, "totally fine") the way casual modern speech sometimes does — for textbook/formal Japanese, pair 全然 with a negative verb.
- Memory tip: 全（entire）+ 然（so）= "entirely so" — reinforced by a negative verb, "not at all."

**24. 意外と（いがいと）— 意外地**
- 例文: このテストは意外と簡単でした。
- 翻譯: 這個考試意外地簡單。
- Common mistake: Confusing 意外と ("surprisingly," describing an unexpected outcome) with the similar-sounding 以外（いがい, "except for") — different kanji, unrelated meaning.
- Memory tip: 意外（unexpected）+ と = "in an unexpected way."

**25. 特に（とくに）— 特別**
- 例文: 私は日本の料理が、特に寿司が好きです。
- 翻譯: 我喜歡日本料理，特別是壽司。
- Common mistake: Placing 特に at the very start of an unrelated sentence instead of right next to the specific item being singled out.
- Memory tip: 特（special）+ に（adverbial particle）= "especially, particularly."

## ② Kanji (5): 長, 短, 強, 少, 多

**長 — Meaning: 長 (long)**
- On-reading: チョウ
- Kun-reading: なが.い
- Words: 長い（this lesson）, 部長（ぶちょう, department manager — Lesson 28）, 社長（しゃちょう, company president — Lesson 28）
- Example sentence: 会議はいつも長いです。
- Stroke order: 8 strokes — top short horizontal stroke first, then the long flowing strokes forming the character's body, finishing with the hook at the bottom.
- Chinese difference: Same meaning as Traditional Chinese 長, and the Japanese standard form stays close to the Traditional shape rather than Simplified 长. As a job-title suffix (部長, 社長), Japanese uses it exactly the way Chinese uses 長/长 for "the head of..."

**短 — Meaning: 短 (short)**
- On-reading: タン
- Kun-reading: みじか.い
- Words: 短い（this lesson）, 短期（たんき, short-term）
- Example sentence: この映画は短いです。
- Stroke order: 12 strokes — write the left radical 矢 (arrow) first, then build the right-side 豆 (bean) shape.
- Chinese difference: Same shape and meaning as Chinese 短. The 矢 (arrow) radical is a shared hint in both languages — "short" historically relates to measuring with an arrow's length.

**強 — Meaning: 強 (strong)**
- On-reading: キョウ
- Kun-reading: つよ.い
- Words: 強い（this lesson）, 勉強（べんきょう, study — Lesson 6）
- Example sentence: 彼は体が強いです。
- Stroke order: 11 strokes — left-side 弓 (bow) first, then the right-side components added afterward.
- Chinese difference: Same shape/meaning as Chinese 強/强. Notice this same kanji already appeared in 勉強 (study) back in Lesson 6 — a reminder that kanji compounds don't always combine their individual meanings literally, in either language.

**少 — Meaning: 少 (few, little)**
- On-reading: ショウ
- Kun-reading: すく.ない、すこ.し
- Words: 少し（this lesson）, 少ない（すくない, few — this lesson's reading）, 少年（しょうねん, boy）
- Example sentence: お金が少ないです。
- Stroke order: 4 strokes — small dot-like stroke first (upper area), then a vertical stroke down the center, finishing with two short flanking strokes.
- Chinese difference: Same shape/meaning as Chinese 少. In Chinese, 少 often pairs with age (少年, youth; 少女, girl) as well as quantity; in Japanese, 少 overwhelmingly signals "small in amount" (少ない, 少し) — don't default to reading it as "young."

**多 — Meaning: 多 (many)**
- On-reading: タ
- Kun-reading: おお.い
- Words: 多い（おおい, many — the natural antonym pairing with 少ない）
- Example sentence: この町は人が多いです。
- Stroke order: 6 strokes — write the top 夕-shape first, then the identical 夕-shape directly beneath it.
- Chinese difference: Same shape/meaning as Chinese 多. 多い／少ない (many/few) is one of the most fundamental adjective pairs in Japanese, just as 多／少 form a basic opposite pair in Chinese.

## ③ Grammar (3 points)

### Grammar 1 — 〜という〜 (called, named...)
**Meaning:** Introduces the name of something/someone — "a thing/person called X."
**Structure:** Noun/quoted phrase + という + Noun.
**Examples:**
1. 「元気」という言葉を知っていますか。(Do you know the word "元気"?)
2. 田中さんという人が来ました。(A person named Tanaka came.)
3. 寿司という食べ物は世界で有名です。(The food called sushi is famous worldwide.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- ❌ Omitting という and just placing the two nouns side by side (❌田中さん人 → ⭕田中さんという人) — という is required to link a name to the noun it describes.

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. Do you know a place called Kyoto?
2. I have a friend named Yuki.
3. What is a food called ramen?

### Grammar 2 — 〜ておきます (to do in advance)
**Meaning:** Doing something in advance/preparation for later benefit, or deliberately leaving something in a convenient state.
**Structure:** Verb te-form + おきます.
**Examples:**
1. 旅行の前に、切符を買っておきます。(I'll buy the ticket in advance before the trip.)
2. 部屋を掃除しておきます。(I'll clean the room beforehand.)
3. 単語を覚えておいてください。(Please memorize the vocabulary in advance.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- ❌ Dropping the おきます nuance and just using the plain verb, losing the "in preparation for later" meaning that おきます specifically adds.
- ❌ Confusing おきます (to place/do in advance) with 起きます (Lesson 6, "to wake up") — identical reading おき, unrelated meaning and kanji.

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. I'll make a reservation in advance.
2. Please read this book in advance.
3. I'll buy a map beforehand.

### Grammar 3 — 動詞のば形（条件）(ba-conditional)
**Meaning:** A general conditional, "if X, then Y" — often used for natural consequences, general truths, and advice.
**Structure:**
- U-verbs: change the final -u sound to -e, add ば (読む→読めば, 買う→買えば).
- Ru-verbs: drop る, add れば (見る→見れば, 食べる→食べれば).
- い-adjectives: drop い, add ければ (安い→安ければ).

**Examples:**
1. 練習すれば、上手になります。(If you practice, you'll become skillful.)
2. 安ければ、買います。(If it's cheap, I'll buy it.)
3. 時間があれば、旅行に行きたいです。(If I have time, I want to travel.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- ❌ Misconjugating u-verbs ending in う (❌買あば → ⭕買えば — the final sound shifts from う to え, not あ).
- ❌ Using ば interchangeably with なら/たら (taught in later lessons) without noticing ば's stronger "general/natural consequence" nuance.

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. If you study, you'll pass. (使用 合格する)
2. If it's not expensive, I'll buy it.
3. If you turn left, there's a bank. (使用 曲がる, 銀行)

## ④ Reading

Read the passage aloud:

コーヒーと紅茶では、コーヒーのほうが好きです。紅茶よりコーヒーのほうが強いですから。でも、妹はコーヒーが全然好きではありません。妹はいつも紅茶を飲みます。

**Comprehension questions:**
1. 私はコーヒーと紅茶と、どちらが好きですか。(Which does the speaker prefer, coffee or tea?)
2. どうしてコーヒーのほうが好きですか。(Why does the speaker prefer coffee?)
3. 妹は何が好きですか。(What does the younger sister like?)

**Answer key:**
1. コーヒーのほうが好きです。(Coffee.)
2. コーヒーのほうが強いからです。(Because coffee is stronger.)
3. 紅茶が好きです。(She likes tea.)

## 🇹🇼 Chinese Notes

- **少（少し／少ない）**: In Chinese, 少 commonly pairs with age (少年 "youth," 少女 "girl") in addition to quantity. In Japanese, 少 overwhelmingly signals "small in amount" (少ない, 少し) — don't default to reading it as "young" in Japanese compounds.
- **全然**: Casual modern speech sometimes pairs intensifiers with positive statements freely. In standard/textbook Japanese, 全然 is traditionally paired only with a negative verb (全然分かりません, "not at all understand") — pairing it with a positive statement (全然大丈夫です) is a newer, very casual usage that beginners should avoid in formal writing.
- **強（in 勉強）**: The character 強 ("strong") appears inside 勉強 ("study," Lesson 6) even though studying isn't literally about "being strong" — a reminder that kanji compounds don't always combine their individual meanings literally, in either Japanese or Chinese.
