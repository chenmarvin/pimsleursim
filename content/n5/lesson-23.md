### Word-by-word details

**1. 嬉しい（うれしい）— 開心**
- 例文: プレゼントをもらって、嬉しいです。
- 翻譯: 收到禮物很開心。
- Common mistake: Confusing 嬉しい (happy about something specific that just happened) with 楽しい (fun, an ongoing enjoyable activity, L8) — 嬉しい is a reaction, 楽しい describes the activity itself.
- Memory tip: The word sounds like a small "wow!" — a burst of joy reacting to good news.

**2. 悲しい（かなしい）— 難過**
- 例文: 映画を見て、悲しいです。
- 翻譯: 看了電影覺得難過。
- Common mistake: Confusing 悲しい (sad, a general emotion) with 寂しい (#3, lonely, a more specific feeling of isolation) — they overlap loosely in translation but describe different flavors of feeling.
- Memory tip: 悲 stacks 非 (wrong/negative) over 心 (heart) — "a heart in the wrong place" = sadness.

**3. 寂しい（さびしい）— 寂寞**
- 例文: 一人で寂しいです。
- 翻譯: 一個人很寂寞。
- Common mistake: Using 寂しい to mean simply "sad" — it specifically implies loneliness or a sense of isolation, not sadness from any cause.
- Memory tip: Related in feel to 静か (quiet, L8) — a quiet, empty feeling inside.

**4. 恥ずかしい（はずかしい）— 害羞、丟臉**
- 例文: 友達の前で歌って、恥ずかしいです。
- 翻譯: 在朋友面前唱歌，很不好意思。
- Common mistake: Thinking 恥ずかしい only means "shameful" (a serious, formal sense) — in daily use it covers everything from mild shyness to embarrassment, much more casual than English "shame."
- Memory tip: Associate the word with a hot, blushing feeling in your chest — this is the adjective for that exact sensation.

**5. 怖い（こわい）— 可怕**
- 例文: あの映画は怖いです。
- 翻譯: 那部電影很可怕。
- Common mistake: Confusing 怖い (something is scary/frightening, a reaction to a thing) with 心配な (#6, worried about an outcome) — different objects of the feeling.
- Memory tip: 忄(heart radical) + 布 (cloth, phonetic hint) — a heart trembling like cloth in the wind.

**6. 心配な（しんぱいな）— 擔心**
- 例文: 試験が心配です。
- 翻譯: 擔心考試。
- Common mistake: Forgetting 心配 is a na-adjective, needing な before a noun (心配な問題, not ❌心配問題) — beginners often drop な the way Chinese needs no linking particle.
- Memory tip: 心 (heart) + 配る (distribute, L22) = "heart being spread thin" = worry.

**7. 大丈夫（だいじょうぶ）— 沒關係、還好**
- 例文: 大丈夫ですか。—はい、大丈夫です。
- 翻譯: 你還好嗎？——嗯，沒事。
- Common mistake: This is the single biggest false-friend trap in N5 for Chinese speakers — assuming 大丈夫 relates to its literal Chinese-character meaning ("a great/manly husband"). In Japanese it simply means "alright/fine/no problem" and has nothing to do with masculinity. See the Chinese Notes section below for the full explanation.
- Memory tip: Memorize 大丈夫 as one fixed phrase meaning "it's okay" — do not try to derive its meaning from the individual kanji.

**8. 安心する（あんしんする）— 放心**
- 例文: 試験に合格して、安心しました。
- 翻譯: 考試合格了，放心了。
- Common mistake: Confusing 安心する (the emotional shift into relief after worry is resolved) with 大丈夫 (a state of being fine, not necessarily tied to relief) — 安心する is the change in feeling, 大丈夫 is the resulting condition.
- Memory tip: 安 (peaceful, shares a root with 安い, L11) + 心 (heart) = "a peaceful heart" = relief.

**9. 怒る（おこる）— 生氣**
- 例文: 先生が怒りました。
- 翻譯: 老師生氣了。
- Common mistake: Confusing 怒る (おこる, to get angry) with 起こる (おこる, identical reading, different kanji, "to occur/happen") — same pronunciation, unrelated meanings; the kanji and context distinguish them.
- Memory tip: 怒 has 心 (heart) at the bottom, under 奴 — a heart under pressure boiling into anger.

**10. 思う（おもう）— 覺得、想**
- 例文: 明日は雨だと思います。
- 翻譯: 我覺得明天會下雨。
- Common mistake: Dropping the quoting particle と before 思います (❌ 雨です思います) — と is required to mark exactly what you think.
- Memory tip: The very common pattern 〜と思います ("I think that...") is built directly on this verb — learn them together.

**11. 分かる（わかる）— 明白、懂**
- 例文: 日本語が分かります。
- 翻譯: 我懂日文。
- Common mistake: Using を instead of が with 分かる (❌ 日本語を分かります) — 分かる is one of a small group of verbs that take が for the thing understood, not を.
- Memory tip: Same 分 kanji as 分ける (L22, to divide) — understanding is like things becoming clearly "sorted out" in your mind.

**12. 知る（しる）— 知道**
- 例文: 山田さんを知っていますか。
- 翻譯: 你認識山田先生嗎？
- Common mistake: Using plain 知ります to mean "I know" — 知る describes the moment of *finding out*; the ongoing state of "knowing" always requires 知っています (a state-ています, like Lesson 22 Grammar 3).
- Memory tip: Pair with 分かる — 分かる is understanding a fact/concept, 知っている is knowing information or people.

**13. 忘れる（わすれる）— 忘記**
- 例文: 名前を忘れました。
- 翻譯: 忘記名字了。
- Common mistake: Confusing 忘れる (forgetting information from memory) with 落とす (L22, dropping a physical object) — Japanese keeps these separate even though casual Chinese can blur "forgetting" and "leaving something behind."
- Memory tip: Contains 心 (heart) under 亡 (disappear) — a feeling "dying away" from the heart = forgetting.

**14. 覚える（おぼえる）— 記住**
- 例文: 歌を覚えます。
- 翻譯: 記住這首歌。
- Common mistake: Confusing 覚える (deliberate, active memorization) with 思い出す (L22, spontaneous recall of something already learned) — different points in the memory process.
- Memory tip: Think "look at it again and again until it sticks" = memorize.

**15. 意見（いけん）— 意見**
- 例文: あなたの意見は何ですか。
- 翻譯: 你的意見是什麼？
- Common mistake: Confusing 意見 (a reasoned opinion/viewpoint) with 気持ち (#25, an emotional feeling) — both loosely translate as "how you feel," but 意見 is about thoughts/judgments.
- Memory tip: 意 (idea/intention) + 見 (see, L6) = "how you see something in your mind" = opinion.

**16. 理由（りゆう）— 理由**
- 例文: 遅刻の理由は何ですか。
- 翻譯: 遲到的理由是什麼？
- Common mistake: Confusing 理由 (a reason/cause) with 意見 (#15, an opinion/stance) — beginners sometimes answer a "why" question with an opinion instead of a cause.
- Memory tip: 理 (logic) + 由 (cause/origin) = "the logical origin" = reason.

**17. 大切な（たいせつな）— 重要**
- 例文: 家族は大切です。
- 翻譯: 家人很重要。
- Common mistake: Confusing 大切な (important/precious, emotional or personal value) with 必要な (#18, necessary, practical requirement) — something can be 大切 without being 必要, and vice versa.
- Memory tip: 大 (big) + 切 (cut) = "something so important you'd be cut/hurt to lose it."

**18. 必要な（ひつような）— 必要**
- 例文: 水は必要です。
- 翻譯: 水是必需的。
- Common mistake: Using 必要 interchangeably with 大切 (#17) — 必要 is about practical necessity (you can't do without it), not emotional value.
- Memory tip: 必 (must) + 要 (need) = "must have" = necessary.

**19. 泣く（なく）— 哭**
- 例文: 悲しくて、泣きました。
- 翻譯: 因為難過而哭了。
- Common mistake: Confusing 泣く (なく, to cry, for people) with 鳴く (なく, identical reading, "to chirp/cry," for animals, L24 preview) — same pronunciation, different kanji and subject.
- Memory tip: Contains 氵(water radical) — tears are water.

**20. 笑う（わらう）— 笑**
- 例文: 友達と笑いました。
- 翻譯: 和朋友一起笑了。
- Common mistake: Assuming 笑う only means loud laughing — context can also make it mean a gentler "smile"; don't over-narrow its meaning.
- Memory tip: Contains 竹 (bamboo) on top — imagine bamboo leaves rustling like laughter shaking through them.

**21. 驚く（おどろく）— 驚訝**
- 例文: 値段に驚きました。
- 翻譯: 對價格感到驚訝。
- Common mistake: Treating 驚く like the other emotion words in this lesson that are adjectives (怖い, 悲しい) — 驚く is a *verb* describing an event ("I became surprised"), not a lasting descriptive trait.
- Memory tip: Contains 馬 (horse) — picture a spooked horse rearing up in surprise.

**22. 緊張する（きんちょうする）— 緊張**
- 例文: 試験の前に緊張します。
- 翻譯: 考試前會緊張。
- Common mistake: Confusing 緊張する (anticipatory nervousness/tension before an event) with 心配な (#6, broader worry about an outcome) — 緊張する is specifically the tension felt right before performing or facing something.
- Memory tip: 緊 (tight) + 張る (stretch/tense) = "stretched tight" = nervous tension.

**23. 感じる（かんじる）— 感覺**
- 例文: 寒いと感じます。
- 翻譯: 覺得冷。
- Common mistake: Confusing 感じる (かんじる, a sensory/emotional perception) with 思う (#10, a cognitive opinion/judgment) — 感じる is about what your senses or emotions register, 思う is about what you conclude.
- Memory tip: Contains 心 (heart) at the bottom — feelings come from the heart.

**24. 楽（らく）— 輕鬆**
- 例文: この椅子は楽です。
- 翻譯: 這張椅子很舒服。
- Common mistake: Confusing 楽（らく, comfortable/easy, no exertion needed）with 楽しい（たのしい, fun, L8）— same kanji, completely different word and reading.
- Memory tip: Same kanji as 楽しい and 音楽 (music, L14) — a broad "ease/enjoyment" root, but 楽 alone (らく) specifically means "comfortable, undemanding."

**25. 気持ち（きもち）— 心情、感覺**
- 例文: 今日はいい気持ちです。
- 翻譯: 今天心情很好。
- Common mistake: Confusing 気持ち (a felt sensation/emotional state in the moment) with 意見 (#15, a reasoned opinion) — 気持ちがいい/悪い describes physical or emotional sensation, not a stance on an issue.
- Memory tip: 気 (spirit/energy, L13) + 持ち (holding, from 持つ, L22) = "what your spirit is holding" = feeling.

## ② Kanji (3): 思, 知, 忘

**思 — Meaning: 思 (think)**
- On-reading: シ
- Kun-reading: おも.う
- Words: 思う, 思い出す (L22), 大丈夫だと思います (fixed useful phrase)
- Example sentence: 明日は晴れだと思います。
- Stroke order: 9 strokes — top 田 (field) first, then 心 (heart) at the bottom; "thoughts spring from the field of the heart."
- Chinese difference: Same character as Chinese 思 (sī), meaning matches closely ("think," 思考／意思 in both). One of the more transparent kanji this lesson — just remember Japanese requires the quoting particle と before 思う, which Chinese doesn't need.

**知 — Meaning: 知 (know)**
- On-reading: チ
- Kun-reading: し.る
- Words: 知る, 知っている, 知識 (preview ちしき)
- Example sentence: 山田さんを知っていますか。
- Stroke order: 8 strokes — left component 矢 (arrow, 5 strokes) first, then 口 (mouth) on the right.
- Chinese difference: Same character as Chinese 知 (zhī), meaning overlaps well ("know," 知道／知識 in both). The real difference is grammatical: Japanese requires the state-form 知っています for "I know," while plain 知ります means "I will find out" — Chinese 知道 has no equivalent present-vs-state distinction.

**忘 — Meaning: 忘 (forget)**
- On-reading: ボウ
- Kun-reading: わす.れる
- Words: 忘れる, 忘れ物 (preview わすれもの)
- Example sentence: 名前を忘れました。
- Stroke order: 7 strokes — top 亡 (disappear, 3 strokes) first, then 心 (heart) at the bottom.
- Chinese difference: Same character as Chinese 忘 (wàng), meaning matches directly ("forget," 忘記 in Chinese uses this same 忘). One of the most transparent kanji in this lesson — shape, meaning, and even the "heart disappearing" logic of the character are identical in both languages.

## ③ Grammar (3 points)

### Grammar 1 — 〜てもいいですか (May I...?)
**Meaning:** Asks for permission to do something.
**Structure:** Verb て-form + もいいですか
**Examples:**
1. この本を借りてもいいですか。(May I borrow this book?)
2. 写真を撮ってもいいですか。(May I take a photo?)
3. この電話を使ってもいいですか。(May I use this phone?)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- ❌ Answering with a mismatched いいえ、いいです — the natural reply pattern is はい、いいですよ (yes, that's fine) or すみません、ちょっと…(sorry, that's a bit difficult) for refusals.
- ❌ Confusing てもいいですか (asking permission for yourself) with てください (L22, asking someone else to do something) — the direction of the request is reversed.

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. May I read this book?
2. May I go home early?
3. May I ask a question?

### Grammar 2 — 〜てはいけません (must not do)
**Meaning:** States a prohibition — an action is not allowed.
**Structure:** Verb て-form + はいけません
**Examples:**
1. ここで写真を撮ってはいけません。(You must not take photos here.)
2. 授業中に話してはいけません。(You must not talk during class.)
3. ここでビールを飲んではいけません。(You must not drink beer here.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- ❌ Inventing a softened negative like ❌ 撮ってもよくないです instead of the standard fixed pattern てはいけません.
- ❌ Treating てはいけません as equally soft as a suggestion — it is a strong, rule-like prohibition ("not allowed"), stronger than a milder "better not to" phrasing at a later level.

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. You must not be late.
2. You must not forget your homework.
3. You must not cry here.

### Grammar 3 — 〜から〜まで (from...to...)
**Meaning:** Marks a starting point and an ending point, for time or place.
**Structure:** [start point] + から + [end point] + まで
**Examples:**
1. 九時から五時まで働きます。(I work from nine to five.)
2. 月曜日から金曜日まで学校があります。(There is school from Monday to Friday.)
3. 台湾から日本まで飛行機で行きます。(I go from Taiwan to Japan by airplane.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- ❌ Using only から and dropping まで when both the start and end matter — から and まで work as a pair; using only half leaves the sentence sounding incomplete.
- ❌ Reversing the word order (❌ まで〜から) — Japanese strictly follows start-から-end-まで, unlike the more flexible "從...到..." ordering possible in Chinese.

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. I study from seven to nine every night.
2. The bank is open from nine to three.
3. From Monday to Sunday, I am busy.

## 🇹🇼 Chinese Notes

- **大丈夫（だいじょうぶ）— the classic false friend**: In Chinese, 大丈夫 (dà zhàng fu) literally means "a great/manly man" — someone of strong, admirable character. In Japanese, 大丈夫 has completely diverged: it simply means "alright / fine / no problem," and is used constantly — asking after someone's health (大丈夫ですか？), reassuring someone (大丈夫です！), or politely declining an offer. There is no connection whatsoever to masculinity or being a "great man" in modern Japanese. This is the single most famous kanji false friend that Chinese-speaking learners encounter in N5, so make sure it's fully internalized here in its home lesson.
- **忘れる**: Same character as Chinese 忘 (wàng), with the same core meaning ("forget," 忘記 in Chinese) — a reassuring, fully transparent kanji that needs no adjustment.
- **楽（らく）vs. 楽（たのしい）**: The identical kanji 楽 carries two very different readings and meanings in Japanese — らく ("comfortable/easy," this lesson) and たのしい ("fun," Lesson 8/14). Chinese 樂/乐 similarly covers both "happiness" and "music," but doesn't split into two clearly different everyday words the way Japanese does — watch for one kanji, multiple unrelated-sounding readings, a recurring pattern throughout this course.
