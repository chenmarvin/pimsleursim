### Word-by-word details

**1. 起きる（おきる）— 起床**
- 例文: 私は毎日六時に起きます。
- 翻譯: 我每天六點起床。
- Common mistake: Chinese speakers sometimes think 起きる only means "to physically stand up," confusing it with 立つ (to stand, later vocabulary) — 起きる specifically means "to wake up / get out of bed."
- Memory tip: Chinese 起 also means "to rise/get up" (起床) — nearly the same meaning; just add きる to make the Japanese dictionary-form verb.

**2. 寝る（ねる）— 睡覺**
- 例文: 私は毎日十一時に寝ます。
- 翻譯: 我每天十一點睡覺。
- Common mistake: Confusing 寝る (the action of going to sleep/lying down) with 眠い (ねむい, "sleepy," a later adjective) — 寝る describes the action, not the feeling.
- Memory tip: 寝 contains 宀 (a roof) — picture "under a roof, lying down" to remember "sleep." Pair it mentally with its opposite, 起きる.

**3. 行く（いく）— 去**
- 例文: 明日、大学に行きます。
- 翻譯: 明天要去大學。
- Common mistake: Confusing the destination marker に (used with 行く／来る／帰る) with the object marker を — ❌ 大学を行きます is a very common beginner error, since English "go to" doesn't map cleanly onto Japanese particles.
- Memory tip: 行 looks like Chinese 行 (to go/walk) — nearly identical meaning, an easy cognate.

**4. 来る（くる）— 來**
- 例文: 田中さんは明日来ます。
- 翻譯: 田中先生明天會來。
- Common mistake: 来る conjugates irregularly (来る→来ます, 来ない, 来て) instead of following a regular pattern — Chinese speakers used to regular verb patterns sometimes mis-conjugate it as ❌ くります instead of きます.
- Memory tip: 来 is close to Traditional Chinese 來 (simplified slightly differently in Japan, close to Simplified Chinese 来) — the meaning "come" is unchanged.

**5. 帰る（かえる）— 回家**
- 例文: 父は毎日六時に帰ります。
- 翻譯: 爸爸每天六點回家。
- Common mistake: 帰る looks like an ichidan (-eru) verb similar to 食べる／寝る, but it is actually an irregular godan verb — learners sometimes wrongly conjugate it as ❌ 帰ます instead of the correct 帰ります.
- Memory tip: Think of 帰る as "returning to base" — pair it with 行く／来る as the third verb of the "go / come / return" trio.

**6. 食べる（たべる）— 吃**
- 例文: 何を食べますか。
- 翻譯: 你要吃什麼？
- Common mistake: Chinese speakers sometimes drop を in casual speech (❌ ご飯食べます) — while common in spoken Japanese too, formal/written N5 sentences should keep the object marker を.
- Memory tip: The kanji 食 depicts a covered food container — same meaning as Chinese 食 (to eat/food), an easy cognate; add べる to form the polite dictionary verb.

**7. 飲む（のむ）— 喝**
- 例文: 何を飲みますか。
- 翻譯: 你要喝什麼？
- Common mistake: A genuine false friend — in Chinese you "eat medicine" (吃藥), but in Japanese you always 薬を飲みます ("drink medicine"), never 食べる. Chinese speakers often say ❌ 薬を食べます by mistake.
- Memory tip: 飲 = 飠 (food/eating radical) + 欠 (an open mouth) — visualize an open mouth taking in liquid.

**8. 見る（みる）— 看**
- 例文: 先生を見ます。
- 翻譯: 看老師。
- Common mistake: 見る covers both "to see" (notice something) and "to watch" (intentionally, like a screen) — Chinese distinguishes 看/看到/看見 more explicitly, so beginners sometimes reach for 見える (to be visible, a different word) when they actually mean the intentional "watch."
- Memory tip: 見 depicts an eye 目 on top of legs 儿 — "an eye that looks" — a very pictographic kanji that also looks exactly like the Traditional Chinese 見 used in Taiwan.

**9. 聞く（きく）— 聽、問**
- 例文: 先生に聞きます。
- 翻譯: 我問老師。
- Common mistake: 聞く means both "to listen" and "to ask." Chinese splits these into 聽 and 問, so learners who only remember "listen" get confused when 聞く appears meaning "ask" (先生に聞きます = "ask the teacher," not "listen to the teacher").
- Memory tip: 聞 = 門 (gate) + 耳 (ear) — "an ear at the gate, listening for news." Note: this differs sharply from modern Mandarin usage of 聞/闻 (see 🇹🇼 Chinese Notes).

**10. 読む（よむ）— 讀、看（書）**
- 例文: 何を読みますか。
- 翻譯: 你在讀/看什麼？
- Common mistake: Confusing the plain action verb 読む (to read) with its potential form 読める ("can read") — beginners sometimes overuse the "can" form when the simple action verb is what's needed.
- Memory tip: 読 contains 言 (speech/words) — matches the Traditional Chinese 讀 (simplified slightly differently in Japan); the meaning "to read" is fully preserved.

**11. 書く（かく）— 寫**
- 例文: 名前を書きます。
- 翻譯: 寫名字。
- Common mistake: Chinese speakers may confuse 書く（かく, to write）with 描く（also read かく, "to draw/paint"）— same reading, different kanji and meaning; context decides which.
- Memory tip: 書 resembles Chinese 書/书 (book/write) — same semantic root; picture a hand writing characters onto a book.

**12. 話す（はなす）— 說、講**
- 例文: 田中さんは日本語を話します。
- 翻譯: 田中先生說日語。
- Common mistake: 話す's basic direct object takes を (日本語を話します). Chinese speakers sometimes reach for で (means/manner) instead, since で also appears with languages in other contexts (日本語で話す, "to speak in Japanese, as a means") — both exist, but beginners conflate the nuance early on.
- Memory tip: 話 = 言 (speech) + 舌 (tongue) — "words from the tongue" = to speak; visually close to Traditional Chinese 話/话.

**13. 買う（かう）— 買**
- 例文: 私は財布を買います。
- 翻譯: 我要買錢包。
- Common mistake: 買う（かう）sounds identical to 飼う（かう, "to keep/raise a pet," different kanji）and is easily confused with 会う（あう, "to meet"）when romanized quickly by beginners — always check the kanji, not just the sound.
- Memory tip: 買 contains 貝 (shell — an ancient currency radical), the same radical found in Chinese 買/买 — directly linking the kanji to "buying with money."

**14. する（する）— 做**
- 例文: 今日、何をしますか。
- 翻譯: 你今天要做什麼？
- Common mistake: する is an irregular verb (します／しない／して) that doesn't follow regular patterns; it also attaches to nouns to form compound verbs (勉強する, 掃除する, 洗濯する) — forgetting to add する after the noun (❌ 勉強です meaning "it is study" instead of "I study") is a common beginner error.
- Memory tip: する has no kanji — memorize it purely by sound as the "default action verb" that turns nouns into verbs.

**15. 勉強する（べんきょうする）— 學習、唸書**
- 例文: 毎日、日本語を勉強します。
- 翻譯: 每天學習日語。
- Common mistake: A major false friend — in Mandarin, 勉強 (miǎnqiǎng) means "to force/reluctantly," a near-opposite feeling. Japanese 勉強 is a completely neutral, ordinary word simply meaning "study," with no sense of reluctance. (See 🇹🇼 Chinese Notes.)
- Memory tip: 勉 (endeavor) + 強 (strong) = "to strive strongly" — the effort of studying, not the feeling of being forced.

**16. 働く（はたらく）— 工作**
- 例文: 兄は会社で働きます。
- 翻譯: 哥哥在公司工作。
- Common mistake: Confusing 働く (to work/labor) with the visually similar 動く (うごく, "to move") — the two kanji differ by only the 人 radical (働 = 動 + person) and are easy to misread when handwritten small.
- Memory tip: 働 = 人 (person) + 動 (move) — "a person moving/laboring." This kanji was actually invented in Japan (国字) and does not exist in Traditional Chinese, unlike most other kanji in this lesson.

**17. 休む（やすむ）— 休息、請假**
- 例文: 明日、会社を休みます。
- 翻譯: 明天要跟公司請假。
- Common mistake: 休む＋を (会社を休みます) means "to take a day off from [work/school]." Chinese speakers sometimes mix up the particle and say ❌ 会社で休みます, which instead means "to rest at the office" — the opposite nuance.
- Memory tip: 休 = 人 (person) + 木 (tree) — "a person resting under a tree," a transparent pictographic kanji identical to Chinese 休.

**18. 会う（あう）— 見面**
- 例文: 明日、友達に会います。
- 翻譯: 明天要跟朋友見面。
- Common mistake: 会う takes the particle に (or と), never を — ❌ 友達を会います is a very common error, since "meet [someone]" feels like a direct-object verb in English/Chinese.
- Memory tip: 会 is the simplified form of 會, matching Simplified Chinese 会 rather than Taiwan's Traditional 會 — visualize two people's paths crossing.

**19. 住む（すむ）— 居住**
- 例文: 私は台湾に住みます。
- 翻譯: 我住在台灣。
- Common mistake: The natural everyday phrase for "I live in ~" is actually 住んでいます (an ongoing-state form taught in a later lesson), not plain 住みます, which can sound more like "I will move there [starting now]." For this lesson, the plain ます-form is used for simplicity.
- Memory tip: 住 = 人 (person) + 主 (main/host) — "the main person [in a place]" = to reside; nearly identical in shape and meaning to Chinese 住.

**20. 使う（つかう）— 使用**
- 例文: 毎日、電話を使います。
- 翻譯: 每天使用電話。
- Common mistake: Chinese 使用 sounds equally natural in casual and formal speech, but in Japanese 使う is the everyday casual verb while 使用する is stiffer/more formal — beginners often overuse 使用する in casual conversation.
- Memory tip: 使 resembles Chinese 使 (to use/make someone do) — same core meaning, an easy cognate kanji.

**21. 洗う（あらう）— 洗**
- 例文: 父は毎週、車を洗います。
- 翻譯: 爸爸每週洗車。
- Common mistake: Chinese 洗 broadly covers washing anything (洗車、洗衣服), but Japanese splits this: 洗濯する is reserved specifically for laundry/clothes, while 洗う is the general-purpose "wash" for other things (cars, dishes, hands).
- Memory tip: 洗 = 氵 (water radical) + 先 (before/ahead) — "water goes first" to clean something, the same water-radical logic as Chinese 洗.

**22. 掃除する（そうじする）— 打掃**
- 例文: 毎週、掃除をします。
- 翻譯: 每週打掃。
- Common mistake: 掃除する is a "noun + する" compound verb, and it can be written either as 掃除します or 掃除をします — both are correct, since する verbs formed from nouns naturally allow を before する. This same pattern applies to 勉強する and 洗濯する, so it's worth internalizing now.
- Memory tip: 掃 (sweep) + 除 (remove) = "sweep away and remove [dirt]" = to clean.

**23. 洗濯する（せんたくする）— 洗衣服**
- 例文: 母は毎週、洗濯をします。
- 翻譯: 媽媽每週洗衣服。
- Common mistake: Chinese casually shortens "do the laundry" to just 洗 (去洗衣服 vs 洗衣), but Japanese never shortens 洗濯する to plain 洗う when specifically talking about laundry — 洗う implies washing a specific single object, while 洗濯する is the fixed term for the household chore of doing laundry.
- Memory tip: 洗 (wash) + 濯 (rinse) — two near-synonym characters doubled up for emphasis, a common Japanese word-building pattern.

**24. 歯を磨く（はをみがく）— 刷牙**
- 例文: 毎日、歯を磨きます。
- 翻譯: 每天刷牙。
- Common mistake: This is a fixed phrase where 磨く means "to polish/brush." Learners sometimes try to substitute 洗う (wash) for teeth-brushing, copying Chinese 洗's broader coverage — but Japanese requires 磨く specifically here.
- Memory tip: 歯 (tooth, containing 止 "stop" above a tooth-like shape) + 磨く (to polish, containing 石 "stone," like polishing with a stone) — memorize the whole phrase 歯を磨く as one fixed unit.

**25. シャワーを浴びる（しゃわーをあびる）— 淋浴、洗澡**
- 例文: 毎日、シャワーを浴びます。
- 翻譯: 每天洗澡（淋浴）。
- Common mistake: シャワーを浴びる is a fixed idiom for "to take a shower." 浴びる alone means "to bathe in / be covered in" (also used in 日光を浴びる, "to bask in sunlight") — learners sometimes drop シャワーを and just say 浴びます, which sounds incomplete without stating what one is bathing in.
- Memory tip: シャワー is the katakana loanword "shower" (from English); always pair it with 浴びる as a fixed set phrase rather than memorizing the two words separately.

## ② Kanji (4): 食, 飲, 見, 聞

**食 — Meaning: 食 (eat, food)**
- On-reading: ショク
- Kun-reading: た.べる
- Words: 食べる (this lesson's vocabulary), 朝食（ちょうしょく, "breakfast" — bonus）
- Example sentence: 友達と食べます。
- Stroke order: Write the short top "roof" strokes first (a small dot, then a sweeping diagonal), then the enclosing outer shape, finishing with the internal strokes at the bottom.
- Chinese difference: Identical to Traditional Chinese 食 (eat/food); the meaning is fully preserved. Note that Chinese compounds sometimes use the variant radical 饣/飠 instead of the full 食 form — Japanese always uses the full form.

**飲 — Meaning: 飲 (drink)**
- On-reading: イン
- Kun-reading: の.む
- Words: 飲む (this lesson's vocabulary), 飲み物（のみもの, "beverage" — bonus）
- Example sentence: 毎日、水を飲みます。
- Stroke order: Write the 氵 (three-dot water radical) on the left first, then the 欠 component (an open-mouth shape) on the right.
- Chinese difference: Identical to Traditional Chinese 飲/饮 (simplified); the meaning "drink" is fully preserved. Note the extra okurigana む needed to form the Japanese dictionary verb, which Chinese doesn't require.

**見 — Meaning: 見 (see, look)**
- On-reading: ケン
- Kun-reading: み.る
- Words: 見る (this lesson's vocabulary), 見学（けんがく, "observation/field trip" — bonus, combines with Lesson 1's 学）
- Example sentence: 先生を見ます。
- Stroke order: Write 目 (eye) on top first, then 儿 (legs) underneath.
- Chinese difference: This is the exact Traditional Chinese form 見, used in Taiwan (Simplified Chinese uses 见 instead) — a rare full match. Unlike 学／万／円 from earlier lessons, which matched Simplified Chinese forms, 見 needs no adjustment at all for Taiwanese learners.

**聞 — Meaning: 聞 (hear, ask)**
- On-reading: ブン、モン
- Kun-reading: き.く
- Words: 聞く (this lesson's vocabulary), 新聞（しんぶん, "newspaper" — bonus）
- Example sentence: 友達の話を聞きます。
- Stroke order: Write the outer 門 (gate) shape first (left side, top, right side, then the two inner "door" strokes), then 耳 (ear) inside it.
- Chinese difference: Same Traditional form as Taiwan's 聞. However, the meaning has drifted: in modern Mandarin, 聞/闻 mostly means "to smell" (難聞, 聞起來), while in Japanese 聞く always means "to hear/listen/ask," never "to smell." This is a genuine false friend — see 🇹🇼 Chinese Notes.

## ③ Grammar (2 points)

### Grammar 1 — 〜があります ("there is/are," inanimate)

**Meaning:** States that an inanimate thing exists, or that the speaker has/owns it.
**Structure:** [Thing] + が + あります
**Examples:**
1. お金があります。(I have money. / There is money.)
2. 財布がありません。(I don't have a wallet.) — the negative form of あります is ありません.
3. 電話番号があります。(There is a phone number. / I have a phone number.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- ❌ 友達があります → ⭕ 友達がいます — Mandarin 有 doesn't distinguish animate from inanimate, but Japanese requires います for people/animals and あります only for things.
- ❌ 財布です (meaning "there is a wallet") → ⭕ 財布があります — です states what something IS (identity/description); あります states that something EXISTS or is possessed. These are not interchangeable.

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. I have money.
2. I don't have a wallet.
3. There is a phone number.
4. I have 500 yen.

### Grammar 2 — 〜に〜があります／います (location + existence)

**Meaning:** States that something or someone exists at a particular place. あります is used for inanimate things; います is used for animate people/animals (reviewed from Lesson 4).
**Structure:** [Place] + に + [Thing/Person] + が + あります／います
**Examples:**
1. ここにお金があります。(There is money here.)
2. あそこに先生がいます。(The teacher is over there.)
3. 台湾に家族がいます。(My family is in Taiwan.)
4. どこに財布がありますか。(Where is the wallet?)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- ❌ 台湾に家族があります → ⭕ 台湾に家族がいます — Mandarin 有 covers both people and things, so this animate/inanimate split must be actively memorized every time.
- ❌ 台湾で家族がいます → ⭕ 台湾に家族がいます — で marks the location of an action (working, eating), while に marks the location of existence; these two location particles are not interchangeable.

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. The teacher is over there.
2. My family is in Taiwan.
3. Where is the wallet?
4. There is money here.

**Answer key (Grammar 1 & 2 exercises):**
1. お金があります。
2. 財布がありません。
3. 電話番号があります。
4. 五百円があります。
5. 先生はあそこにいます。／あそこに先生がいます。
6. 家族は台湾にいます。／台湾に家族がいます。
7. 財布はどこにありますか。
8. ここにお金があります。

## 🇹🇼 Chinese Notes

- **勉強（べんきょう）**: In Mandarin, 勉強（miǎnqiǎng）means "to force" or "reluctantly," carrying a negative feeling. In Japanese, 勉強する is a completely neutral, everyday word simply meaning "to study" — it has no sense of being forced. Don't let the negative Mandarin connotation bleed into your reading of Japanese 勉強.
- **聞（き.く）**: The kanji is identical to Taiwan's Traditional 聞, but the meaning has drifted apart. In modern Mandarin, 聞/闻 mostly means "to smell" (難聞, 聞起來很香), while in Japanese 聞く always means "to hear/listen" or "to ask" — never "to smell." Japanese "to smell" is expressed differently (匂いがする).
- **働（はたら.く）**: Unlike most kanji in this course, which are either identical to or simplified versions of Traditional Chinese characters, 働 is a kanji invented in Japan (国字) and does not exist in Chinese at all — it combines 人 (person) + 動 (move) to mean "to work/labor." Not every kanji you meet in Japanese will have a Chinese counterpart.
