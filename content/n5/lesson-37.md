# 🇯🇵 Japanese for Taiwanese Learners — JLPT N5, Lesson 37

**Theme:** 「将来の夢」(Dreams for the future)

**Objectives**
- Talk about future plans and dreams
- Express what you want someone to do with 〜てほしいです
- Use conditionals to talk about outcomes

### Word-by-word details

**1. つもり — 打算**
- 例文: 来年、日本へ行くつもりです。
- 翻譯: 我打算明年去日本。
- Common mistake: Confusing つもり (one's own intention/plan) with 予定 (Lesson 4, a more fixed, official schedule) — つもりです is what you personally intend, 予定です leans toward something already arranged. Also, つもり follows a plain-form verb, not ます形 (❌行きますつもり).
- Memory tip: Think of つもり as "in my mind's plan" — it lives inside your head, not on a calendar.

**2. 計画（けいかく）— 計畫**
- 例文: 旅行の計画を立てました。
- 翻譯: 我訂了旅行的計畫。
- Common mistake: Using 計画 as a ready-made verb (❌計画ます) instead of 計画する, or forgetting the set phrase 計画を立てる ("to make/set a plan").
- Memory tip: 計 (measure/plan) + 画 (draw/picture) = "drawing out a plan."

**3. 今度（こんど）— 下次**
- 例文: 今度、いっしょに映画を見ましょう。
- 翻譯: 下次一起看電影吧。
- Common mistake: Assuming 今度 always means "now" like 今 — it actually points to a different occasion than right now, either coming up soon (future) or recently past, depending on context.
- Memory tip: 今 (now) + 度 (occasion, from Lesson 36) = "this next occasion."

**4. 将来（しょうらい）— 將來**
- 例文: 将来、医者になりたいです。
- 翻譯: 將來我想成為醫生。
- Common mistake: Mixing up with the more abstract/distant 未来 (the future in general, not core N5 vocab) — 将来 is the natural word for talking about your own personal future/career.
- Memory tip: 将 (about to) + 来 (come, Lesson 4) = "what is about to come."

**5. 夢（ゆめ）— 夢想**
- 例文: 私の夢は先生になることです。
- 翻譯: 我的夢想是成為老師。
- Common mistake: Using 夢 only for sleep-dreams and forgetting it equally means "life goal/aspiration" — both senses share the same word, just like Chinese 夢.
- Memory tip: Same core meaning as Chinese 夢 — an easy cognate word.

**6. 目標（もくひょう）— 目標**
- 例文: 今年の目標は日本語を上手に話すことです。
- 翻譯: 今年的目標是把日語說好。
- Common mistake: Confusing 目標 (a concrete goal you aim for) with 目的 (purpose/reason behind an action, also new this lesson) — 目標 is *what* you're aiming for, 目的 is *why*.
- Memory tip: 目 (eye) + 標 (mark/sign) = "the mark your eyes are fixed on."

**7. 決まる（きまる）— 決定（自動詞）**
- 例文: 旅行の日にちが決まりました。
- 翻譯: 旅行的日期決定了。
- Common mistake: Confusing intransitive 決まる (something becomes decided, no specific decider named) with 決める (Lesson 22, transitive, "to decide something") — 決まる never takes を.
- Memory tip: Same pattern as 変わる (Lesson 36): the まる ending marks a spontaneous, no-agent verb.

**8. 考える（かんがえる）— 思考、考慮**
- 例文: 将来のことをよく考えます。
- 翻譯: 我認真思考將來的事。
- Common mistake: Confusing 考える (deliberate, reasoned thinking) with 思う (Lesson 23, impressions/feelings/opinions) — 考える suits working through a problem, 思う suits stating a belief or feeling.
- Memory tip: The kanji 考 alone is this lesson's third kanji — 考える is literally "考 + える."

**9. 準備する（じゅんびする）— 準備**
- 例文: 旅行の準備をしています。
- 翻譯: 我在準備旅行。
- Common mistake: Not realizing 用意する is a close synonym — 準備する tends to suit bigger/longer preparations (a trip, an event), while 用意する suits smaller, quicker ones (a meal, items on a desk).
- Memory tip: 準 (standard/level) + 備 (equip) = "getting equipped to standard" — prepared.

**10. 楽しみ（たのしみ）— 期待、樂趣**
- 例文: 旅行がとても楽しみです。
- 翻譯: 我非常期待這次旅行。
- Common mistake: Confusing the noun 楽しみ ("something to look forward to," used with です) with the verb 楽しむ (Lesson 14, "to enjoy," an action happening now) — 楽しみです describes anticipation of something not yet here.
- Memory tip: 楽しい (fun, Lesson 8) becomes the noun 楽しみ — "a fun thing yet to come."

**11. 期待する（きたいする）— 期待**
- 例文: いい結果を期待しています。
- 翻譯: 我期待有好結果。
- Common mistake: Treating 期待する as emotionally neutral like English "expect" — in Japanese it carries a hopeful, positive nuance (hoping for something good), unlike a flat prediction.
- Memory tip: 期 (period/time) + 待 (wait) = "waiting through a period [for something good]."

**12. 希望する（きぼうする）— 希望**
- 例文: 大学に入ることを希望しています。
- 翻譯: 我希望能考上大學。
- Common mistake: Confusing with 期待する — 希望する centers on your own wish for an outcome, while 期待する often implies hoping/counting on someone or something else to deliver it.
- Memory tip: 希 (hope) + 望 (wish, gaze afar) = "hoping while looking ahead."

**13. 目的（もくてき）— 目的**
- 例文: 日本語を勉強する目的は何ですか。
- 翻譯: 你學日語的目的是什麼？
- Common mistake: Swapping with 目標 (this lesson) — 目的 answers "why are you doing this," 目標 answers "what exactly are you aiming for."
- Memory tip: 目 (eye) + 的 (target) = literally "eye-target," but remember 目標 is the concrete mark while 目的 is the underlying reason.

**14. チャンス — 機會**
- 例文: これはいいチャンスです。
- 翻譯: 這是一個好機會。
- Common mistake: Not knowing 機会 (きかい, more formal, not core N5 vocab) exists as a native-word equivalent — チャンス is the casual loanword version, both mean "opportunity."
- Memory tip: Straightforward loanword from English "chance" — the pronunciation is close to the original.

**15. チャレンジする — 挑戰**
- 例文: 新しいことにチャレンジしたいです。
- 翻譯: 我想挑戰新事物。
- Common mistake: Assuming it means "to challenge someone [to a fight/contest]" like the literal English — in Japanese it almost always means "to take on a challenge/try something new" for yourself, not confronting another person.
- Memory tip: Loanword from English "challenge," but used reflexively — challenging *yourself*, not others.

**16. 実現する（じつげんする）— 實現**
- 例文: 夢が実現しました。
- 翻譯: 夢想實現了。
- Common mistake: Getting the subject wrong — 夢が実現する ("the dream comes true," 夢 takes が) is the basic pattern; "I made my dream come true" needs the causative 夢を実現させる (previewing Lesson 39's causative form).
- Memory tip: 実 (truth/reality, Lesson 36) + 現 (appear) = "becoming real, appearing as truth."

**17. 叶う（かなう）— （願望）實現**
- 例文: 願いが叶いました。
- 翻譯: 願望實現了。
- Common mistake: Confusing 叶う (specifically for wishes/dreams coming true, and only intransitive) with 実現する (broader — plans, projects, dreams all becoming reality) — 叶う has a more poetic, personal feel and pairs almost exclusively with 夢 or 願い.
- Memory tip: The kanji 叶 looks like 十+口 ("ten mouths") — picture many voices agreeing a wish should come true.

**18. 変える（かえる）— 改變（他動詞）**
- 例文: 考え方を変えることにしました。
- 翻譯: 我決定改變想法。
- Common mistake: Mixing this up with 変わる (Lesson 36, intransitive) — 変える always needs an object marked with を (you change something), while 変わる describes the subject changing by itself.
- Memory tip: Compare directly with the L36 pair 変わる／変える, the same transitive/intransitive pattern seen in 始まる／始める (Lesson 32).

**19. 決断する（けつだんする）— 下決定**
- 例文: 大切な決断をしました。
- 翻譯: 我做了一個重要的決定。
- Common mistake: Using 決断する for trivial everyday choices ("deciding what to eat") — it's reserved for big, often difficult decisions; 決める (Lesson 22) is the everyday, general-purpose word.
- Memory tip: 決 (decide) + 断 (cut off/sever) = "cutting off other options" — a firm, final decision.

**20. 悩む（なやむ）— 煩惱**
- 例文: 進路についてずっと悩んでいます。
- 翻譯: 我一直在為未來的方向煩惱。
- Common mistake: Confusing 悩む (agonizing over a decision/problem, an internal struggle) with 心配する (Lesson 23, worrying about a possible bad outcome) — 悩む is about being stuck choosing, 心配 is about fearing a result.
- Memory tip: Pairs naturally with 相談する (this lesson): 悩んだら、相談しましょう ("if you're troubled, let's talk it over").

**21. 相談する（そうだんする）— 商量**
- 例文: 先生に相談しました。
- 翻譯: 我和老師商量了。
- Common mistake: Using plain 話す (Lesson 6, "to speak") when 相談する ("to consult/seek advice") is meant — 相談する specifically implies seeking guidance about a problem, not just chatting.
- Memory tip: 相 (mutual) + 談 (talk) = "talking it through together."

**22. アドバイス — 建議**
- 例文: 先生からアドバイスをもらいました。
- 翻譯: 我從老師那裡得到了建議。
- Common mistake: Mixing up who gives and who receives — the set patterns are アドバイスをする/くれる ("to give advice") vs. アドバイスをもらう ("to receive advice"); 相談する (this lesson) is the act of *asking* for that advice.
- Memory tip: A straightforward loanword from English "advice."

**23. 提案する（ていあんする）— 提議**
- 例文: 新しい計画を提案しました。
- 翻譯: 我提議了一個新計畫。
- Common mistake: Confusing 提案する ("to propose/suggest an idea," fairly neutral) with お願いする (Lesson 22, "to request a favor") — 提案する offers an idea for consideration; it doesn't ask for a favor.
- Memory tip: 提 (present/offer) + 案 (idea/plan) = "offering up an idea."

**24. 見通し（みとおし）— 展望、預測**
- 例文: 将来の見通しはまだ分かりません。
- 翻譯: 未來的展望還不清楚。
- Common mistake: Overusing 見通し in small daily conversations — it's a fairly formal/business word for long-term "prospects/outlook" (economy, plans, weather trends), not for tiny everyday choices.
- Memory tip: 見る (see, Lesson 6) + 通し (through) = "seeing all the way through" — foresight.

**25. 意欲（いよく）— 意願、幹勁**
- 例文: 彼は勉強に対する意欲が高いです。
- 翻譯: 他對讀書很有幹勁。
- Common mistake: Confusing the formal/written 意欲 with the everyday spoken equivalent やる気 (not core N5 vocab) — 意欲 shows up more in writing and formal speech, やる気 in casual conversation.
- Memory tip: 意 (intention) + 欲 (desire) = "the desire behind one's intentions" — motivation, drive.

## ② Kanji (3): 予, 定, 考

**予 — Meaning: 予 (in advance)**
- On-reading: ヨ
- Kun-reading: —
- Words: 予定 (よてい, Lesson 4), 予習する (よしゅうする, Lesson 16), 予約する (よやくする, Lesson 26)
- Example sentence: 来週の予定を考えます。
- Stroke order: 4 strokes — start with the top-left diagonal stroke, then the hook, followed by the two strokes forming the sweeping bottom-right shape.
- Chinese difference: This matches Traditional Chinese 予, but in Taiwan the character 預 is now far more common for "in advance" compounds (預定, 預習, 預約). Japanese consistently uses the simpler 予 for all of these — don't reach for 預 by habit when writing Japanese.

**定 — Meaning: 定 (decide, fix)**
- On-reading: テイ
- Kun-reading: さだ.める
- Words: 予定 (よてい), 定食 (ていしょく, Lesson 29)
- Example sentence: 旅行の予定が決まりました。
- Stroke order: 8 strokes — write the roof-shaped 宀 across the top first, then build the 疋-like lower component underneath, keeping it centered.
- Chinese difference: Nearly identical in shape and meaning to Chinese 定 ("fixed," "decide") — one of the rare kanji with essentially no gap from Traditional Chinese usage, so it's safe to lean on your existing intuition here.

**考 — Meaning: 考 (think, consider)**
- On-reading: コウ
- Kun-reading: かんが.える
- Words: 考える (this lesson), 参考 (さんこう, "reference" — bonus word)
- Example sentence: よく考えてから決めます。
- Stroke order: 6 strokes — write the top horizontal stroke and slanting stroke first, then the vertical hook through the middle, finishing with the curling stroke at the bottom.
- Chinese difference: Same character as Chinese 考 (as in 考試, "to take an exam"), but Japanese never uses 考 alone to mean "take a test" — that concept uses 試験を受ける (Lesson 16's 試験). In Japanese, 考える is purely the mental act of thinking/considering, without the exam association Chinese speakers may expect.

## ③ Grammar (3 points)

### Grammar 1 — 〜そうです (伝聞, "I heard that...")
**Meaning:** Reports secondhand information — something you heard or read, not your own observation.
**Structure:** Plain form (verb / adjective / noun + だ) + そうです
**Examples:**
1. 天気予報によると、明日は雨だそうです。(According to the forecast, I heard it will rain tomorrow.)
2. 田中さんはもう国へ帰ったそうです。(I heard Tanaka already went back to his country.)
3. 彼は将来医者になるそうです。(I heard he's going to become a doctor in the future.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- Confusing this hearsay 〜そうです (attaches to the *complete* plain form, with だ for nouns/な-adjectives: 雨だそうです) with the appearance 〜そうです from Lesson 29 (attaches to the *stem*, dropping い: 雨が降りそうです, "it looks like it will rain") — they look almost identical but conjugate differently.
- Forgetting that 伝聞そうです cannot itself be negated or put in past tense (❌そうでした) — instead, change the reported clause: 雨だったそうです (I heard it had rained).

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. I heard that tomorrow is a holiday.
2. I heard she is busy.
3. I heard he passed the exam.

### Grammar 2 — 〜と、〜 (automatic result)
**Meaning:** Describes an automatic, inevitable, or habitual result — "whenever/if X, then Y naturally happens."
**Structure:** Plain non-past form + と + result
**Examples:**
1. このボタンを押すと、電気がつきます。(If you press this button, the light turns on.)
2. 春になると、花が咲きます。(When spring comes, flowers bloom.)
3. 右に曲がると、駅があります。(If you turn right, there's a station.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- Using と for a one-off personal plan ("if I have time, I'll go") — と implies something automatic or law-like (machines, nature, directions, habits), not a single controllable choice; use たら or ば instead for personal plans.
- Following と with a volitional, invitation, or command form (❌春になると、旅行しましょう) — と-clauses cannot be followed by these; たら is used instead in such cases.

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. When you turn left, there is a park.
2. If you press this button, it stops.
3. In winter, it gets cold.

### Grammar 3 — 〜たら、〜 (tara-conditional)
**Meaning:** A flexible "if/when" — describes a condition and its result, usable for one-time future plans, requests, and commands.
**Structure:** Verb / adjective past-tense (た) form + ら, + result
**Examples:**
1. 雨が降ったら、行きません。(If it rains, I won't go.)
2. 仕事が終わったら、電話します。(When I finish work, I'll call you.)
3. 将来お金があったら、世界を旅行したいです。(If I have money in the future, I want to travel the world.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- Assuming たら behaves exactly like と and can't be followed by requests/invitations/commands — actually たら is the flexible one; と is the restricted one. Learners often mix up which pattern allows what.
- Thinking the た-shape means the whole sentence is about the past — 〜たら describes a future or hypothetical condition despite its "past-looking" form.

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. If it rains tomorrow, I will stay home.
2. When you arrive, please call me.
3. If I have time, I want to consult with the teacher.

## ④ Reading

私には将来の夢があります。医者になって、みんなを助ける仕事をしたいです。子供の時から、その夢を持っています。今、大学で一生懸命勉強しています。友達によると、医者になるのはとても難しいそうです。でも、諦めないで頑張るつもりです。もし試験に合格したら、両親はとても喜ぶと思います。将来のことを考えると、少し心配になりますが、楽しみでもあります。夢が叶うように、これからも準備をしていきます。

**Comprehension questions:**
1. 「私」の将来の夢は何ですか。
2. 友達によると、医者になるのはどうですか。
3. 試験に合格したら、両親はどうなると思いますか。

**Answer key:**
1. 医者になって、みんなを助ける仕事をすることです。
2. とても難しいそうです。
3. とても喜ぶと思います。

## 🇹🇼 Chinese Notes

- **予（予定／予約／予習）**: Matches Traditional Chinese 予, but in Taiwan today the character 預 is used for these same "in advance" compounds (預定, 預約, 預習). Japanese always uses the simpler 予 — don't substitute 預 when writing Japanese.
- **考（考える）**: Shares the character used in Chinese 考試 ("to take an exam"), but Japanese 考える never carries that exam meaning — it purely means "to think/consider." Don't expect an exam-related nuance just because the character looks familiar from 考試.
- **叶う vs. 實現/如願**: The single kanji 叶 doesn't correspond to any common standalone Traditional Chinese word learners already know, and its meaning ("a wish comes true") is narrower than 実現する ("a plan/project becomes reality") — keep the two Japanese words distinct even though both can be translated as "come true" in English.
