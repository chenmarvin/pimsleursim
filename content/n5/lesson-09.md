### Word-by-word details

**1. ご飯（ごはん）— 飯、餐**
- 例文: 昨日、晩ご飯にご飯を食べました。
- 翻譯: 昨天晚餐我吃了飯。
- Common mistake: Assuming ご飯 only means "cooked rice grains" — it also means "a meal" in general (as in 朝ご飯/昼ご飯/晩ご飯), so ご飯を食べました can simply mean "I ate a meal."
- Memory tip: ご (polite prefix) + 飯 (cooked rice, same idea as Chinese 飯) — the ご is now a permanently fused part of the everyday word.

**2. パン（ぱん）— 麵包**
- 例文: 昨日、朝ご飯にパンを食べました。
- 翻譯: 昨天早餐我吃了麵包。
- Common mistake: パン comes from Portuguese "pão," not from any Chinese root — don't try to connect it to 麵包 by sound; it must simply be memorized as its own katakana word.
- Memory tip: Short and easy — say it like the English word "pan" (as in a frying pan) to fix the sound in memory.

**3. 水（みず）— 水（冷水）**
- 例文: 水を飲みました。
- 翻譯: 我喝了水。
- Common mistake: Japanese 水 refers specifically to cold or room-temperature plain water — hot water is a separate word (お湯), unlike Chinese 水 which can be generic. Don't ask for 水 when you want hot water.
- Memory tip: Same kanji as Chinese 水; just remember the "cold" nuance attached to it in Japanese.

**4. お茶（おちゃ）— 茶**
- 例文: お茶を飲みますか。
- 翻譯: 你要喝茶嗎？
- Common mistake: Dropping the お prefix and just saying 茶 sounds unnatural in everyday conversation — お茶 is the standard everyday form, unlike some other お-nouns where the お is optional.
- Memory tip: お (polite prefix) + 茶 (tea, identical to Chinese 茶).

**5. コーヒー（こーひー）— 咖啡**
- 例文: 朝ご飯にコーヒーを飲みました。
- 翻譯: 早餐我喝了咖啡。
- Common mistake: Dropping the long vowel marks (ー) — コヒー instead of コーヒー changes the rhythm and sounds foreign/unclear to native ears.
- Memory tip: From English "coffee" — each ー lengthens the vowel before it; say koo-hii slowly.

**6. 肉（にく）— 肉**
- 例文: 昨日、肉を食べませんでした。
- 翻譯: 昨天我沒有吃肉。
- Common mistake: Unlike some Chinese contexts where unqualified 肉 can lean toward "pork" by default, Japanese 肉 alone is a general term — specify 牛肉／豚肉／鶏肉 for a particular type of meat.
- Memory tip: Identical kanji and meaning to Chinese 肉 — an easy, low-risk word.

**7. 魚（さかな）— 魚**
- 例文: 魚が好きです。
- 翻譯: 我喜歡魚。
- Common mistake: The everyday kun-reading さかな is irregular-looking, so learners try to guess a reading based on the on-reading ギョ — for the food item itself, さかな is what's used in daily speech.
- Memory tip: The kanji 魚 visually resembles a fish shape (head at top, tail fins at the bottom) — same shape as Chinese 魚.

**8. 野菜（やさい）— 蔬菜**
- 例文: 野菜を食べました。
- 翻譯: 我吃了蔬菜。
- Common mistake: Confusing 野菜 (vegetable) with 果物 (fruit) right after learning both — remember 菜 relates to "dish/greens," just like in Chinese 菜.
- Memory tip: 野 (field) + 菜 (vegetable/dish) = "field vegetables."

**9. 果物（くだもの）— 水果**
- 例文: 果物は美味しいです。
- 翻譯: 水果很好吃。
- Common mistake: The reading くだもの is a special whole-word reading, not built from the on-readings of 果 or 物 — don't try to sound it out piece by piece.
- Memory tip: 果 (fruit) + 物 (thing) = "fruit things."

**10. 卵（たまご）— 蛋**
- 例文: 朝ご飯に卵を食べました。
- 翻譯: 早餐我吃了蛋。
- Common mistake: Seeing the alternate kanji 玉子 (also read たまご, often used in cooking contexts like 玉子焼き) and thinking it's a different word — both spellings share the same reading and core meaning.
- Memory tip: The kanji 卵 looks like a small cluster of eggs — a handy visual mnemonic.

**11. 牛乳（ぎゅうにゅう）— 牛奶**
- 例文: 牛乳を飲みます。
- 翻譯: 我喝牛奶。
- Common mistake: Treating 牛乳 and ミルク as always interchangeable — 牛乳 is the everyday word for plain drinking milk, while ミルク more often refers to milk added to coffee/tea or baby formula.
- Memory tip: 牛 (cow) + 乳 (milk) = "cow's milk," the same logic as Chinese 牛奶.

**12. ジュース（じゅーす）— 果汁**
- 例文: 昨日、ジュースを飲みませんでした。
- 翻譯: 昨天我沒有喝果汁。
- Common mistake: In Japanese, ジュース usually implies fruit juice specifically, not "any sweet drink" the way the English word "juice" is sometimes used more loosely.
- Memory tip: From English "juice" — say it as ju-u-su.

**13. ビール（びーる）— 啤酒**
- 例文: 父はビールが好きです。
- 翻譯: 我爸爸喜歡啤酒。
- Common mistake: Dropping the long vowel turns ビール (beer) into ビル (building) — a classic N5 listening trap, so always hold the long ー sound.
- Memory tip: Long ー = alcohol (ビール); short = building (ビル). Picture a tall glass to remember the "long" version.

**14. 朝ご飯（あさごはん）— 早餐**
- 例文: 朝ご飯を食べましたか。
- 翻譯: 你吃早餐了嗎？
- Common mistake: Treating 朝 and ご飯 as separable in this context — 朝ご飯 is one fixed compound word meaning "breakfast," not "morning" plus "meal" used loosely.
- Memory tip: 朝 (morning) + ご飯 (meal) = "morning meal."

**15. 昼ご飯（ひるごはん）— 午餐**
- 例文: 昼ご飯はレストランで食べました。
- 翻譯: 我在餐廳吃了午餐。
- Common mistake: Assuming 昼 alone means "lunch" — 昼 alone means "daytime/noon"; ご飯 must stay attached to specifically mean "lunch."
- Memory tip: 昼 (noon) + ご飯 (meal) = "noon meal."

**16. 晩ご飯（ばんごはん）— 晚餐**
- 例文: 晩ご飯は何ですか。
- 翻譯: 晚餐是什麼？
- Common mistake: Assuming 夜ご飯 is the only/more "correct" alternative — 晩ご飯 (and 夕ご飯) are the standard textbook words for dinner; this lesson's title phrase is worth memorizing as a fixed set expression.
- Memory tip: This lesson's theme phrase — 晩 (evening) + ご飯 (meal).

**17. お菓子（おかし）— 點心、零食**
- 例文: 子供はお菓子が好きです。
- 翻譯: 小孩喜歡零食。
- Common mistake: Assuming お菓子 only means "Western-style candy" — it broadly covers snacks and sweets of all kinds, including traditional Japanese sweets (和菓子).
- Memory tip: お (polite prefix, customary here) + 菓子 (confection).

**18. 塩（しお）— 鹽**
- 例文: 塩を使いました。
- 翻譯: 我用了鹽。
- Common mistake: Expecting the kanji to look exactly like traditional Chinese 鹽 — Japanese 塩 is a simplified/different form even though the meaning is identical.
- Memory tip: 塩 contains 土 (earth) on the left — salt was historically harvested by evaporating earth/seawater.

**19. 砂糖（さとう）— 糖**
- 例文: 砂糖が好きですか。
- 翻譯: 你喜歡糖嗎？
- Common mistake: Confusing the reading さとう with the common Japanese surname 佐藤 (also さとう) — context (food vs. a person's name) tells them apart.
- Memory tip: 砂 (sand) + 糖 (sugar) = sugar visualized as fine, sand-like grains.

**20. 醤油（しょうゆ）— 醬油**
- 例文: 魚に醤油を使いました。
- 翻譯: 我在魚上用了醬油。
- Common mistake: Trying to pronounce 醤油 based on the Mandarin reading jiàngyóu — the Japanese reading しょうゆ shares the kanji but sounds completely different; never guess a Japanese reading from Mandarin pronunciation.
- Memory tip: 醤 (sauce/paste) + 油 (oil) = "sauce oil" — the meaning matches Chinese 醬油 closely even though the sound doesn't.

**21. 箸（はし）— 筷子**
- 例文: 箸を使いますか。
- 翻譯: 你用筷子嗎？
- Common mistake: Using plain 箸 in polite conversation when お箸 is more natural and commonly heard — similar politeness pattern to お茶／お菓子.
- Memory tip: The 竹 (bamboo) radical sits on top of 箸 — chopsticks are traditionally made of bamboo.

**22. お腹が空く（おなかがすく）— 肚子餓**
- 例文: お腹が空きました。
- 翻譯: 我肚子餓了。
- Common mistake: Inserting を instead of が (❌ お腹を空きました) by analogy with "eating a food" patterns — 空く is intransitive here, so the subject-marking が is required.
- Memory tip: お腹 (stomach) + が空く (becomes empty) = "the stomach becomes empty" = hungry.

**23. 喉が渇く（のどがかわく）— 口渴**
- 例文: 喉が渇きました。
- 翻譯: 我口渴了。
- Common mistake: Confusing 渇く (かわく, to become thirsty, used for living beings) with the different kanji 乾く (かわく, to dry out, used for objects like laundry) — same reading, different kanji and usage.
- Memory tip: Pair お腹が空く (hungry) and 喉が渇く (thirsty) together as a matching set — both use が, never を.

**24. 美味しい（おいしい）— 好吃、美味**
- 例文: この魚は美味しいです。
- 翻譯: 這條魚很好吃。
- Common mistake: Trying to guess a reading from the kanji shapes 美・味 — it must always be read as one whole word, おいしい, never built up on-reading by on-reading.
- Memory tip: 美 (beautiful) + 味 (taste) + しい (i-adjective ending) = "beautifully tasting" = delicious.

**25. まずい（まずい）— 難吃**
- 例文: このコーヒーはまずいです。
- 翻譯: 這咖啡很難喝。
- Common mistake: Using まずい as a direct, casual translation of "not tasty" without realizing how blunt/rude it sounds — avoid saying it about food someone personally cooked for you.
- Memory tip: Written only in hiragana in everyday use (no common kanji) — pair it mentally as the direct opposite of 美味しい.

## ② Kanji (2): 魚, 空

**魚（fish）**
- Meaning (Chinese): 魚
- On-reading: ギョ
- Kun-reading: さかな
- Words: 魚（さかな, fish — already known）, 魚屋（さかなや, fish shop — bonus）, 人魚（にんぎょ, mermaid — bonus, note the on-reading ぎょ appearing here）
- Example sentence: 魚を食べます。
- Stroke order tip: Start with the top hook-like vertical stroke, then add the horizontal strokes that build the body segment, and finish with the four bottom dots (like the 灬 "fire" radical) drawn left to right.
- Chinese difference: 魚 is identical to Traditional Chinese 魚, including the four-dot bottom — Taiwanese learners get an exact visual match, unlike Simplified Chinese 鱼, which drops those strokes.

**空（sky, empty）**
- Meaning (Chinese): 空
- On-reading: クウ
- Kun-reading: そら、あ.く、す.く
- Words: 空く（すく, part of お腹が空く — already known）, 空（そら, sky — bonus）, 空気（くうき, air — bonus）
- Example sentence: お腹が空きました。
- Stroke order tip: Write the 穴 (cave/hole) radical first — the top dot, then the slanting "roof" strokes and the sides — then finish with the 工 shape at the bottom (horizontal, vertical, horizontal).
- Chinese difference: Same character as Chinese 空, and "empty/sky" as a meaning overlaps directly. However, Japanese attaches three distinct readings depending on grammatical role — クウ (on, in compounds like 空気), そら (kun noun, "sky"), and あ.く／す.く (kun verb, "to become empty") — far more reading complexity than the single Mandarin kōng. お腹が空く specifically uses the すく reading, an irregular okurigana pattern that must simply be memorized as a set phrase.

## ③ Grammar (2 points)

### Grammar 1 — 動詞のました形（肯定・過去）polite past affirmative verbs

**Meaning:** Describes an action that was completed in the past, in the polite (です／ます) register.
**Structure:** Verb ます-stem + ました (the ます ending is replaced entirely by ました). Example conversions: 食べます→食べました、飲みます→飲みました、します→しました、きます→きました。

**Examples:**
1. 昨日、晩ご飯を食べました。(I ate dinner yesterday.)
2. 今日、コーヒーを飲みました。(I drank coffee today.)
3. 友達が来ました。(My friend came.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- ❌ 食べますました → ⭕ 食べました — ました *replaces* ます entirely; it does not attach on top of it.
- ❌ Mixing the casual plain-past form 食べた into an otherwise polite です／ます sentence — stay consistently polite by using ました, not the plain-form た, in this course's register.
- ❌ Assuming that a past-time word like 昨日 alone is enough to signal past tense, and leaving the verb in ます form (❌ 昨日、食べます) — unlike Chinese, where a time word can carry most of the "pastness," Japanese still requires the verb itself to be conjugated to ました.

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. I drank tea yesterday.
2. My friend came today.
3. I worked yesterday.
4. I ate breakfast today.

### Grammar 2 — 動詞のませんでした形（否定・過去）polite past negative verbs

**Meaning:** Describes an action that did NOT happen in the past, in the polite register.
**Structure:** Verb ます-stem + ませんでした (the ません ending is extended with でした). Example conversions: 食べません→食べませんでした、飲みません→飲みませんでした。

**Examples:**
1. 昨日、肉を食べませんでした。(I didn't eat meat yesterday.)
2. 今日、コーヒーを飲みませんでした。(I didn't drink coffee today.)
3. 昨日、学校へ行きませんでした。(I didn't go to school yesterday.)

**Common mistakes (Chinese speakers):**
- ❌ ませんました (garbling the two pieces together in the wrong order) → ⭕ ませんでした — memorize ませんでした as one fixed chunk, not two separately reorderable parts.
- ❌ Stacking a second でした on an already-negative form (❌ 食べませんでしたでした) — でした attaches only once.
- ❌ Confusing verb ませんでした with the copula's past-negative pattern used for nouns/adjectives (e.g. ではありませんでした) — ませんでした shown here is specifically for verbs; noun/adjective past-negative uses a different construction, not interchangeable with this one.

**Exercises (translate into Japanese):**
1. I didn't drink milk yesterday.
2. She didn't eat vegetables today.
3. I didn't go to the bank yesterday.
4. My father didn't work yesterday.

**Answer Key (Grammar 1 & 2):**

- G2-1. 昨日、牛乳を飲みませんでした。

- G2-3. 昨日、銀行へ行きませんでした。

## 🇹🇼 Chinese Notes

- **水 vs. Chinese 水**: Japanese 水 specifically means cold or plain (unheated) water. Hot water is a completely separate word, お湯, which does not exist as a distinct concept in everyday Chinese usage of 水. Don't ask for 水 when you mean hot water.
- **醤油（しょうゆ）**: The kanji is essentially the same as Chinese 醬油 and the meaning matches exactly, but the Japanese reading しょうゆ has no phonetic relationship to Mandarin jiàngyóu — resist the temptation to pronounce it the Mandarin way.
- **空 reading complexity**: Chinese 空 has one core reading (kōng) across its uses. Japanese 空 splits into クウ (on, in compounds like 空気), そら (kun, "sky" as a noun), and あ.く／す.く (kun, "to become empty," as a verb). The idiom お腹が空く specifically borrows the すく reading — an irregular pairing worth memorizing as a fixed phrase rather than deriving logically from the kanji's other readings.
