# Japanese for Taiwanese Learners (JLPT N5 → N1) — Course Requirements

Numbered requirements. Append new ones with the next sequential number; do not renumber existing ones.

**REQ-1. Level progression.** Beginner level must cover hiragana and katakana before introducing kanji.

**REQ-2. Lesson structure.** Each lesson contains, in order:
1. Vocabulary (20 words)
2. Kanji (5 characters)
3. Grammar (2–3 points)
4. Reading passage
5. Listening script
6. Speaking / shadowing
7. Writing practice
8. Quiz
9. Review

**REQ-3. Vocabulary entry format.** For every vocabulary word, use this format:

```
日本語    Reading    中文    English
学生      がくせい    學生    student
```

Then per word:
- Example sentence (Japanese), e.g. わたしは学生です。
- Translation (Chinese), e.g. 我是學生。
- Common mistakes
- Memory tips

**REQ-4. Kanji entry format.** Instead of just memorizing characters, for every kanji include:
- Character, e.g. 学
- Meaning (Chinese), e.g. 學
- On-reading, e.g. ガク
- Kun-reading, e.g. まなぶ
- Words using it, e.g. 学生, 大学, 学校
- Example sentence, e.g. 学校へ行きます。
- Stroke order tips
- Chinese differences (how the kanji differs from the Chinese hanzi it resembles)

**REQ-5. Grammar entry format.** Not just rules — always follow this pipeline:
Meaning → Structure → Examples → Common mistakes → Exercises

Example (〜は〜です):
- Meaning/structure: X is Y
- Examples: わたしはマービンです。/ あなたは先生です。/ 彼は医者です。
- Exercise: translation prompts, e.g. "I am Taiwanese." / "She is a teacher."

**REQ-6. Reading passage format.** At the beginning level, passages are very short.
Example:
わたしはマービンです。台湾人です。毎日日本語を勉強します。

Followed by comprehension questions, e.g.:
- Where is Marvin from?
- What does he study?

**REQ-7. Speaking / shadowing format.** Shadowing exercise: learner repeats a short set of lines aloud after the audio, e.g.:
わたしはマービンです。
台湾人です。
よろしくお願いします。

**REQ-8. Listening script format.** Listening sections must provide a written script/transcript; a read-aloud (TTS) feature is used to read that script aloud for the listening practice.

**REQ-9. Writing practice format.** Includes stroke-order tracing for kanji. Learner writes 5–10 sentences and the app corrects them.

**REQ-10. Quiz format.** Includes multiple choice, fill-in-the-blank, and listening comprehension questions.

**REQ-11. Review cadence.** Every five lessons, insert a review lesson covering everything so far, with no new material.

**REQ-12. N5 curriculum scope.** Approximately 40 lessons. Each regular lesson introduces 20 vocabulary, 5 kanji, 2 grammar points (per REQ-2/REQ-3/REQ-4/REQ-5); every 5th lesson is a review lesson (per REQ-11) with no new vocab/kanji/grammar. After lesson 40, the learner will know approximately:
- 800 vocabulary words
- 100 kanji
- 80 grammar points

**REQ-13. N4 curriculum scope.** About 60 lessons, same structure as N5 (per REQ-12). By end of N4, cumulative totals reach approximately:
- 1,500 vocabulary words
- 300 kanji
- 170 grammar points

**REQ-14. N3 reading materials.** Starting at N3, reading passages (per REQ-6) draw from authentic material types:
- NHK News Easy
- Blogs
- Emails
- Manga

**REQ-15. N2 reading materials.** At N2, reading passages (per REQ-6) draw from:
- Newspapers
- Business Japanese
- Technical articles

**REQ-16. N1 reading materials.** At N1, reading passages (per REQ-6) draw from:
- Editorials
- White papers
- Legal documents
- Technical manuals
- Academic papers

**REQ-17. Lesson header.** Each lesson opens with a Theme (e.g., 「はじめまして」) and an Objectives bullet list of what the learner will be able to do.

**REQ-18. Kanji Review mini-check.** The kanji section (REQ-4) ends with a short reading check combining the lesson's kanji into words already introduced (e.g., 日本, 学生, 日本人, 大学生).

**REQ-19. Listening pacing.** Listening scripts (REQ-8) are read once slowly, then once at natural speed.

**REQ-20. Speaking repetition.** Shadowing lines (REQ-7) are repeated three times each; the goal is to speak naturally without reading.

**REQ-21. Quiz breakdown by pillar.** Quizzes (REQ-10) include one question per content pillar: vocabulary, grammar, reading, and kanji (in addition to the multiple-choice/fill-in-blank/listening question types already specified).

**REQ-22. Expected Learning Outcomes section.** After the Review section (REQ-11), each lesson includes a separate "Expected Learning Outcomes" checklist of skills gained (distinct from the completion checklist in Review).

**REQ-23. Chinese Notes section.** Beyond a typical JLPT textbook, each lesson adds a 🇹🇼 "Chinese Notes" section highlighting differences between Japanese and Chinese usage of shared characters/words (false friends), e.g. 大丈夫 meaning differently in Japanese than in Chinese.

**REQ-24. Toggleable hiragana furigana.** All Japanese words and sentences throughout the course must be accompanied by hiragana (furigana) readings, and this display must be able to be toggled on/off dynamically (a UI/app-level setting, not baked into static content).

## Appendix: Worked Example — N5 Lesson 1

See below for a full worked example illustrating all of the above requirements.

### 🇯🇵 Japanese for Taiwanese Learners — JLPT N5, Lesson 1

**Theme:** 「はじめまして」(Nice to meet you)

**Objectives**
- Introduce yourself.
- Say who you are.
- Ask someone's name.
- Read your first five kanji.

#### ① Vocabulary (20 words)
| # | Japanese | Reading | 中文 | English |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 私 | わたし | 我 | I, me |
| 2 | あなた | あなた | 你 | you |
| 3 | 学生 | がくせい | 學生 | student |
| 4 | 先生 | せんせい | 老師 | teacher |
| 5 | 会社員 | かいしゃいん | 公司職員 | office worker |
| 6 | 日本 | にほん | 日本 | Japan |
| 7 | 台湾 | たいわん | 台灣 | Taiwan |
| 8 | 中国 | ちゅうごく | 中國 | China |
| 9 | 人 | ひと／じん | 人 | person |
| 10 | 名前 | なまえ | 名字、姓名 | name |
| 11 | です | です | 是 | to be |
| 12 | はい | はい | 是、好的 | yes |
| 13 | いいえ | いいえ | 不是 | no |
| 14 | よろしく | よろしく | 請多指教 | please treat me well |
| 15 | 初めまして | はじめまして | 初次見面 | Nice to meet you |
| 16 | 何 | なに／なん | 什麼 | what |
| 17 | ～さん | さん | 先生／小姐（敬稱） | Mr./Ms. |
| 18 | ～人 | ～じん | ……人 | nationality |
| 19 | 友達 | ともだち | 朋友 | friend |
| 20 | 大学 | だいがく | 大學 | university |

Memory note example: 学生（がくせい）— 學=學, 生=生, easy because kanji nearly identical to Chinese.

#### ② Kanji (5): 人, 日, 本, 学, 生
Example entry —
**人**: Meaning "Person". Readings: ジン, ニン, ひと. Words: 日本人, 台湾人, 一人. Example sentence: 私は台湾人です。
**日**: Meaning "Sun/Day/Japan". Readings: ニチ, ジツ, ひ. Words: 日本, 今日, 日曜日. Memory: looks like the sun.
(本、学、生 continue the same way.)

**Kanji Review** (mini reading check at end of kanji section): Can you read these? 日本／学生／日本人／大学生

#### ③ Grammar (2 points)
**Grammar 1 — ～は～です** ("A is B"). Structure: A は B です. Examples: 私は学生です。/ 私は台湾人です。/ マービンさんは先生です。
Common mistake (Chinese speakers): ❌ 私学生です → ⭕ 私は学生です — the topic marker は is almost always required.

**Grammar 2 — ～ですか** (question form). Examples: 学生ですか。→ はい、学生です。／いいえ、学生ではありません。

#### ④ Reading
Read the passage aloud:
マービンです。台湾人です。会社員です。日本語を勉強しています。よろしくお願いします。
Questions: ①マービンさんはどこの人ですか。②会社員ですか。③日本語を勉強していますか。

#### ⑤ Listening
Read once slowly, then at natural speed.
A: はじめまして。マービンです。
B: はじめまして。私はゆきです。よろしくお願いします。
Questions: ①男の人の名前は何ですか。②女の人の名前は何ですか。

#### ⑥ Speaking (Shadowing)
Repeat three times: 私は台湾人です。／私は会社員です。／よろしくお願いします。
Goal: speak naturally without reading.

#### ⑦ Writing
Translate into Japanese (5 sentences): "I am Taiwanese." / "I am a student." / "I am Marvin." / "Are you a teacher?" / "Nice to meet you."

#### ⑧ Quiz
One question per pillar shown: Vocabulary (meaning of 会社員), Grammar (fill-in-the-blank particle), Reading (translate a sentence), Kanji (read 学生).

#### ⑨ Review
Checklist of the day's 9 components (vocabulary, kanji, grammar, reading, listening, speaking, writing, quiz — reviewed).

#### Expected Learning Outcomes
Checklist of skills gained, e.g.: introduce yourself, ask someone's name, read ~15–20 basic words, recognize 5 kanji, understand A は B です.

#### 🇹🇼 Chinese Notes (added beyond a typical JLPT textbook)
Highlights differences between Japanese and Chinese usage of shared characters/words. Example: 大丈夫 does not mean the same as its Chinese reading — in Japanese it means "all right / okay / no problem" depending on context.
